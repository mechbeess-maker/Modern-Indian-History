Struggle Begins !

# Factors which favored rise of Nationalism:#

  1. Collision of the interests: Many of the Indian society members started to realize that their interest would never be served in their country till the time b's are ruling India. Just because the presence of B's, Indian intellengsia class skipped away form the administration, Indian trade and industry was completely despoiled and peasants were highly oppressed.
  2. Administrative unification of India: British authority unified India administratively for ease of administration but it also led to unification of grievances of people. Now they started to identify B's as their common enemy and reason behind their grievances.
  3. Development of Modern transport and communication: 
    • Development of railways was done with the British perspective. But up to some extent it facilitated in the growth of nationalism. First railway line was laid in 1853 b/n Bombay to Thane. By 1900, 25000 miles of railway line was laid down. In 1865, a British officer Edwin Arnold made a prophecy “Railways may do for India what dynasties have never done, what the genius of Akbar could not achieve by his good governance not the cruelty of Tipu Sultan. Railways may make India truly a nation”.
    • In b/n 1848 and 1854, modern postal system {→ by Dalhousie} and telegraph system was initiated which also facilitated in rise and growth of nationalism.
  4. Inaugural of Western Education: This proved to be highly momentous for the rise and growth of nationalism. Now the renaissance literature of Europe was available in India and Indians were inspired by thought process and ideas of European thinkers like: John Locke, Voltaire, Montesquieu, Thomas Hobb, JJ Rousseau, Jeremy Bentham, JS Mill etc. Even ideas of French revolution like liberty, freedom and Equality also started to inspire Indians. Many of the Indians had first hand experience of working in Europe. once they returned back to India they found condition to be very slavish. They started to ask basic questions with British authority that what is the intention of British rule in India.
  5. Growth of vernacular Press: The first newspaper of India was Bengal Gazette by James Augustus Hickey in 1780. After which many other newspapers in English language were also released. The growth of vernacular press in India picked up pace after revolt of 1857. Large number of vernacular newspapers like: Amrita Baazar Patrika {Ghosh Brothers}, Bengali {Girish Chandra Ghosh and Surendranath Banerjee}, Indu Prakash {Vishnu Parshuram Pandit}, The Hindu {G. Subramania Iyer, M. Veeraraghavachariar, and N. Subba Rao Pantulu}, Keshari & Mahratta {Bal Gangadhar Tilak}, Indian Mirror {Devendra Nath Tagore and Manmohan Ghosh}, Young India {Mahatma Gandhi}, New Weal (Commonweal) {Annie Besant} etc. These all newspapers started to publish exploitative aspect of British rule.
  6. Rise of New Middle Class (Educated Middle Class): The new middle class of India was purely created on the basis of education. After getting their appointments in administration, they started to reflect that why India was colonized by handful of British. They introspected and realized that it is due to many rigid divisions in the society. They felt that to throw out British rule in India all the Indians will have to come together and forge a new identity of being Indian.
  7. Inspiration from India's glorious past: Large number of European scholars came to India and they deeply studied Indian literature and upheld the superior wisdom mentioned in these texts. After discovery of IVC, British authority maintained that one of the most advanced civilization of the world existed in Indian subcontinent.
  8. Inspiration from Revolutionary Activities in other parts of world:
    1. On the ruins of Spanish and Portuguese empire many nation states emerged in South America. 
    2. The role of revolutionaries like Garibaldi and Mazzini was often quoted by Indian leaders as an inspiration.
    3. In 1905, small Japan defeated big Russia. These revolutionary activities broke the myth of invincibility of big powers.
  9. Racial discrimination policy: British followed the policy of racial discrimination since the inception of their rule in India. For eg:
    • Though civil services was open to Indians but practically it was very difficult. The maximum age was reduced to 19 years by 1876.
    • Ilbert Bill Controversy: Viceroy Ripon introduced this bill which was aimed at ending Judicial Disqualification on the basis of racial distinction. in other words if bill gets passed Indian judges will be entitled to try European convicts also. But introduction of this bill led to huge protest in British parliament and finally it was modified from its original version. 
    • → Now it became very clear to Indians that justice and fair play cannot be expected form B's where interest of their own community is also involved at the same point of time. 
  10. Economic Critic of Colonialism:
    • One of the biggest contribution of early nationalist of India was economic critique of colonialism
    • DBN was the 1st and foremost personality who criticized british colonialism. The 2nd important was RC Dutta who wrote “Economic History of India”,
    • Other notable leaders included leaders like GKG, MGR, GSI, PC Ray, GV Joshi.
    • These leaders were able to trace colonization of indian economy in all its 3 layers i.e. Trade, Industry, and Finance.
      • Trade:
        • There was one way free trade for BEIC traders and their products were free of any customs duty but when indian products entered into any other market they were imposed with very high customs duty which made their products uncompetitive.
      • Industry:
        • Right form the inception of british rule, british policy was all about  supporting the british industries and ensure uninterrupted raw material supply to british industries form India.
        • As a result hardly any industry developed in India. And moreover if at all any industry was operating, it faced dearth of raw material.
      • Finance:
        • Financial imperialism is the worst stage of economic colonization. 
        • In India it happened through development of railways. 
        • The entire capital for railway development was brought from Britain and british investors demanded hefty security of investment as such it created vested interest which further perpetuated british colonialism in India.
    • Drain of Wealth:
      • Dada Bhai Naoroji was the high priest of theory of drain of wealth. He maintained that britain is not only draining India of its resources but also bleeding India.
      • British officer John Sullivan who was president of Board of Taxes Madras beautifully explained drain mechanism He said “Our system very much acts like a sponge, drawing uup all the good things form the banks of Ganges and squeezing them down to the bank of Thames”.
      • DBN explained 3 components of drain of wealth:
        1. Remittances - It was salaries and savings of british officials which they sent to Britain.
        2. Home Charges of Indian government in Britain - It included Pensions of british officials, interest on public debt and any expense of indian govt in Britain which was to be paid form indian revenue.
        3. Excess of export over import - for which India got no material return. It was mainly due to export of raw material and not the manufactured goods.
      • DBN further calculated the drain amount to more than half of the GDP of India, 1/3rd of total savings and more then total land revenue of India.
      • Drain of wealth theory an economic critic had a greater merit to be easily understood by the nation of peasants. No other thought can better provoke Indians then the thought that they are being taxed in India so that people living in far land can live in comfort.

# Foundation of Congress:#

After the suppression of 1857 revolt many regional organizations came up to create political awareness in certain parts of country the important regional organization were:

  1. Landholders’ Society (1837-38):
    • The founders were Dwarkanath Tagore and Radhakant Deb.
    • The objective was to safeguard the rights of zamindars.
  2. British Association of Bengal:
    • Founded by George Thomson in 1843.
    • Objective: Safeguarding the rights of the common public. 
  3. British India Association:
    • The above two organizations were merged to create British India Association.
    • The objective is to safeguard the rights of the common public and zamindars. 
  4. Bombay Association: (1852)
    • Founder: Dadabhai Naoroji.
    • It was created for demanding legislative assembly for Indians.
  5. East India Association (1866):
    • Created in London. By Dadabhai Naoroji.
    • It calculated the Drain of Wealth.
  6. East India Association of Calcutta: (1876)
    • Founder: Surendra Nath Banerjee and Anand Mohan Bose.
    • Purpose: To protest against the reactionary measures of Viceroy Lytton and demand political advancement of Indians. 
  7. Madras Mahajan Sabha: (1884) 
    • M V Raghavacheriar, P Charlu, G Subramaniyan Iyer
    • To increase the number of Indians in legislative assemblies. 
  8. Bombay Presidency Association (1885)
    • Founders: Pherozshah Mehta, K T Telang, Badaruddin Tyabji.
    • To demand more political rights for India's, and to increase their numbers in legislative assemblies.

Foundation of INC:

  • By 1884, the leaders of all these organizations realized that they are not effective in their regional capacity and time has come to create an All India organization to create effective pressure on foreign rule.
  • As a result in 1885, INC was founded. Its creation was facilitated by a retired british civil servant Alan Octavian Hume. 
  • 1st meeting of congress was convened at Gokuldas Tejapal Sanskrit college at Bombay on 28th Dec.
  • This session was presided by Womesh Chandera Banerjee.

Theory of Safety Valve and lightening conductor:

  • After the foundation of congress, many theories were proposed regarding its creation. The most popular was ToSV, which was backed by LLR. As per this theory, INC was created by AO Hume as a Safety Valve to British empire in India. 
  • Prima Facie it was supposed to address the grievances of rising middle class in India but its original intention was to avoid any serious revolt like 1857 against B's. In a way it wanted to safeguard British empire so that British rule would continue for forever in India.
  • Leaders like GKG rejected it and maintained that congress was not the sole idea of AO Hume but also Indian nationalist. He further added that in the prevailing circumstances, congress is the best option to try for redressal of grievances of Indians. 
  • He said while being in INC, Indian nationalist can act as lightning conductor for the freedom of India.

# 1st Phase of Freedom Struggle (1885 to 1905) {aka Moderate Phase}:#

Soon after the creation of congress, Indian freedom struggle entered into its very 1st phase. Which is aka moderate phase of freedom struggle. The initial leaders of congress included names like DBN, RC Dutt, PC Ray, MG Ranade, GGK, GV Joshi, G Subramaniya Iyer, SNB, KT Telang, Pherozshah Mehta,  Badruddin Taiyyabji, Anand Mohan Bose, P Anand Charly, V Raghava Cheriar.

These leaders adopted moderate method of struggle whereby, the believed in Prayers, Petitions, Memorials to British Authorities and Constitutional Agitation. They never believed in bypassing any law but to work within the four walls of law.

They didn't had faith in the capacity of masses and they never initiated any mass movement. They wanted to create political awareness in society and also to spread political education.

In the legislative councils they demanded Political and material advancements of Indians and they also demanded reforms in Civil Services, simultaneous conduct of Civil Service Exam in India and also increasing the number of Indians in the Civil service.

Their most important contribution was economic critic of colonialism

  • They were able to trace colonization of Indian economy in all its three layers i.e. Trade, Industry and Finance.
  • They also explained drain of wealth from India and how Britain was depriving Indians from their own resources.

# 2nd Phase of Freedom Struggle (1905-1915)#

→ aka Extremist Phase / Passive Resistance Phase.

On 19th July 1905, Viceroy Curzon announced partition of Bengal on communal line. He created East Bengal with Muslim majority and west bengal with Hindu majority population (earlier, he made a statement → “Bengal united is a strengthen and bengal divided will fall of in many ways”)

East bengal had areas like todays Bangladesh and assam. and west bengal included bengal, Odisha and Bihar. 

The decision was implemented on the 16th of October 1905. With the partition of bengal, Indian freedom struggle entered into its 2nd phase which is called as extremist or passive resistance phase.

At this point of time some of the leaders of congress talked about going ahead with mass movement or passive resistance strategy {prayers and petitions must be backed by the pressure of passive resistance}.

These leaders were popularly called as extremist leaders of congress with names like BGT, LLR, Bipin Chandra Pal, Aurobindo Ghosh and VO Chidambaram Pillai. The greatest representative of this thought was Bal Gangadhar Tilak.

Bal Gangadhar Tilak

  • He was a Mathematics teacher at Fergusson College Pune.
  • BGT was the most famous extremist leader at this time. He joined congress in 1890s. 
  • To increase his popularity he started to celebrate Ganpati Festival (1893) and Shivaji Festival (1895).
  • He also released two magazines i.e. Keshari (Marathi) and Mahratta (English).
  • In 1897, for the 1st time he was given Jail term of 18 months for writing provocative article in Keshari. When he was released from Jail, he popularized slogan “Swaraj is my birth right and i shall have it”. Originally it was coined by Francis Baptist (Kaka Baptiste).
  • It was Tilak who maintained that the Hindi language written in Devanagari script must be used as a National Language

The pressure created by extremist leader resulted into onset of passive resistance movement.

Annual session of INC 1905 (Banaras)

  • It was presided by GKG. In this session he approved beginning of Swadeshi and Boycott movement.
  • It said "Indians will boycott Manchester clothes and Liverpool salt".
  • Both Swadeshi and boycott were interlinked and based on the development of Atmashakti (Self-Reliance).

Swadeshi Movement:

  • Majority of the developments of Swadeshi Phase was in the field of culture and education which is as follows:
    • RNT wrote “Amar Sonar Bangle” which later became national anthem of Bangladesh.
    • Dakshinaranjan Mitra an emotional song “Thakurmar Jhui” which is even popular till this date.
    • Bankim Chandra Chatterjee's song “Vande Mataram” became song of freedom struggle.
    • Abanindranath Tagore broke the European tradition of painting and started to paint on the indian themes. His iconic painting was “Bharat Mata”. In this painting “Bharat Mata” was depicted holding a piece of cloth, bunch of paddy, rudraksha beads and a book. These items were indicative of basic necessities of indians and spiritual and secular knowledge.
    • Abanindranath Tagore along with his Japanese friends went on to create “Oriental Society of Art” which started to offer scholarship to bright students. Nanadalal Bose was the 1st recipient of this scholarship and later on he went on to become the most famous painter of INC.
    • Near about 800-900 National schools were created a large number of bright students were sent to Japan and Germany to study engineering and technology.
    • In Maharashtra, Sakharam Ganesh Deushkar, wrote “Desher Katha” in 1904 in which he warned against "Colonial state Hypnotic Conquest of Mind". Later when partition of bengal happen this text became very famous and within few months >2000 copies were sold. Later in 1910, British banned this book.
    • Acharya PC Ray created "Bengal Chemical Factory" (Pharmaceutical)
    • VO Chidambaram Pillai created “Bharat Swadeshi Steam Navigation Company”.

Limitations of Swadeshi Boycott movement:

  • It was not able of garner the support of the muslim masses particularly in bengal mainly due to:
    1. Divide and rule policy followed by British authority particularly in bengal.
    2. Reactionary leadership among muslim masses in bengal.
      • Foundation of All India Muslim League - 1906:
        • It was founded by Nawab Sallimullah of Dhaka and Agha Khan and it was supported by then viceroy Minto.
        • On the day of Its foundation ML maintained that they created this platform to address the grievances of Muslim community. Their intention is not to create communal disharmony in society but only to promote muslim interest. 
        • They also pledged their complete loyalty to British authority.

Annual Session of INC 1906 (Calcutta):

  • Before the session differences emerge b/n moderates and extremist leaders on the question of boycott. Extremist leaders wanted all out boycott of British manufactured goods and services. OTOH, Moderates wanted to stick to boycott of Manchester clothes and Liverpool salt. 
  • Extremist leaders wanted their own president in Calcutta session but moderates leaders announced the name of DBN who was equally respected by both sides.
  • DBN tried to consoliate b/n the two groups and gave 5 resolutions:
    1. Swadeshi
    2. Boycott
    3. Swaraj (self rule in line with British colonies in Australia and Canada)
    4. National education
    5. Annulment of Partition of Bengal.
  • These resolutions were not able to solve the differences b/n extremist and moderate leaders.

Annual Session of INC 1907 and Surat Split:

  • Originally session was planned at Nagpur which was hub of extremist leaders but on a very short notice moderate leaders changed the venue of session to Surat. So that Tilak will not be able to contest elections.
  • {Aurobindo Ghosh brought 3000 congress people from Calcutta but Bombay being closer moderated called 7000 of their guys}
  • In this session moderate leader Rash Bihari Ghosh was elected as congress president. 
  • {When Tilak was speaking, Rash bihari Ghosh did not allowed him to speak much, but tilak continued. Many moderate guys approached tilak with sticks. Fight started → mayhem in the assembly. A chappal was thrown to Rash bihari but he doged and it hit Pherozshah Mehta🤣 (he was sitting beside SNB) → Pherozshah Mehta took this to his heart. → did not allowed extremist to rejoin INC, in fact he was the biggest block → 1916 when he died → within a week both sides were united 🤣}
  • Unfortunately in this session leaders of both the factions Faught fiercely with each other.
  • Moderate leaders captured the entire machinery of congress and expelled the extremist leaders form the congress. This episode is famously k/as Surat split of congress.

# British Administration after 1857:#

Queen Victoria's Proclamation 1858:

  • On 1st Nov 1858, GG Canning read out Queen Victoria proclamation at a durbar held at Allahabad, which formally ended Mughal Rule and initiated rule of British crown.
  • Queen's proclamation disclaimed any intention of conquest in India. 
  • It assured that rights of Indian Princely states would be respected. 
  • It further said that British crown will follow the policy of Justice, benevolence and religious toleration.
  • It further added that customs traditions rights usages faith etc of indians will be respected and Indians will be entitled public service and education without discrimination on some grounds.

GoI Act 1858: → {This was in haste, Therefore was not elaborate}

  1. It ended the company's rule and so as the abolition of CoD and BoC.
  2. GG of India → also designated as Viceroy of India (as a viceroy he is representative of British crown and as a GG he is Administrative head of India)
  3. British cabinet minister Secretary of State was made responsible for GoI.
  4. To assist SoS a 15 member Council of India was created (later it became Imperial Legislative Council / Central Legislative Council)

Indian Councils Act 1861:

British authority realized after the suppression of 1857 revolt that it is very important to include indians in the process of rule making and as such it led to the beginning of representative institutions in which indians will also be the part.

  1. Indian members were included into central legislative council as non-official members but British retained official majority in the council.
  2. It also recommended creation of provincial legislative council as such it was created in Bengal (1862), NWFP (1866), and Punjab (1897).
  3. Viceroy was canning was entitled to initiate portfolio system where by he created different departments of administration. 
  4. It recommended establishment of High courts as such 3 HC's were created at Bombay, Madras and Calcutta. The supreme court at Calcutta was subsumed within HC.
  5. GG was entitled to issue ordinances on important matters w/o the connivance of his council.

Indian Councils Act 1892:

  1. It increased the number of Indians (non-official members) in both central and provincial councils but official majority was retained in both of it. 
  2. To fill some of non-official seats indirect election method was followed but it is to be noted that no where in the act word “election” was mentioned.
  3. Indian members were allowed to discuss budget and address a question to British.

Morley-Minto Reforms / Indian Council Act 1909 / GoI Act 1909:

  1. It decided to give majority to non-official members in provincial legislative council. 
  2. But it retained official majority in central council.
  3. For the appointment of non-official members an electoral college was created the members of it were basically privileged and rich class of India eg. rich traders, merchants, local representatives, teachers, professors etc. Income was the criteria to be included in the electoral college.
  4. Indian members of the council were not only allowed to discuss the budget but also vote on separate budget items. They were also allowed to ask supplementary question to Executive. 
  5. It announced Separate Electorate for Muslim Masses. Moreover the income criteria of muslim masses was kept way lower then the other communities.
    • As per the provision of separate electorate, in particular constituency candidates would be the muslims and so is the voters.
  6. One indian member was to be added into viceroy ex council and Satyendra Prasad Sinha was the 1st indian member.

Moderate leaders were highly dissatisfied with 1909 act. They expected Parliamentary form of responsible government in India but SoS Morley clearly mentioned that he has absolutely no intention of introducing Parliamentary form of government in India. 

Indian leaders conveyed this dis-pleasure to higher British authorities in Britain. As such in 1911, Grand Delhi Durbar was held and for the very 1st time British monarch King George V visited India to participate in durbar. 

He announced annulment of Partition of bengal but also the creation of two new stated i.e. Bihar and Odisha. It was also decided that capital of British India would be transferred form Calcutta → New Delhi.

Two British architect, Sir Edwin Lutyns and Herbert Backer were entrusted with the task of developing new architecture at Delhi.

# Revolutionary Nationalism Phase -1 (1905-1915):#

Pre-Revolutionary events (before 1905):

  1. Vasudev Balwant Phadke (1876-77)
    • often regarded as father of indian armed rebellion after the revolt of 1857.
    • Around 1876 he created a group of Ramosi's, Bhil's, Mahar's and Dhang's and marched towards pune. He expelled British authority from pune and captured its administration for a week.
    • Later he was arrested and tried in court.
    • He was defended by GV Joshi, but unfortunately 1883 his death happened due to hunger strike.
  2. Chapekar brothers (1897):
    • In 1890s there was severe spread of Bubonic Plague in Bombay presidency. 
    • At the same point of time two British officers WC Rand and Lt. Ayerst were involved in many atrocities against people who were affected with plague.
    • At this time, motivated by the article of Tilak written in his magazine Keshari, Chapekar brothers killed these two British officers.
    • It was the 1st example of political assassination in India.
    • The wrath of this killing fell on Tilak and he was handed jail term of 18 months.

Reasons for rise of revolutionary nationalism:

  1. Partition of bengal on communal line was a trigger to initiate revolutionary nationalism.
  2. Youth of India never coincided with the methods of moderated leaders {efforts of Moderates did not result in any substantial change in the Indian political scenario} they considered moderate methods are like begging for their own rights in their own country. 
  3. There was high dissatisfaction with other British administrative policies and also the tactics of divide and rule.
  4. Indiscriminate killing of Indians by the British of frivolous charges. Youth of India regarded extremist leaders as their role model. When Tilak was arrested and deported to Mandalay Jail youth became determined to take the revenge.
  5. They also drew inspiration from outside India, whereby a very small Japan defeated imperialist Russia.

Bengal:

  • Anushilan Samiti:
    • Initially it was established as a body building society were youth were also exchanging ideas of nationalism. 
    • After the partition of bengal it acquired revolutionary character and within a short span of time 3 branches of it were was created i.e. Midnapore (led by Gyanendra nath Bas), Calcutta (Barindra Ghosh {brother of Aurobindo Ghosh}, Bhupendra nath Dutta {brother of Swami Vivekananda}) and Dhaka (Pulin Bihari Das).
    • The objective of Anushilan Samiti was to kill unpopular British officers, informers and traitors. 
    • They also conducted Swadeshi Dacoities to raise funds for purchasing arms and to initiate an armed fight with British.
    • Activities of Anushilan Samiti:
      • Bright youth members of Anushilan were sent to Japan and Germany to learn the art of bomb making (Hemant Kanungo and Ullaskar Datta {💌 story}).
      • They created a bomb making factory at Maniktalla, calcutta.
      • Two revolutionaries Prafulla Chaki and Khudiram Bose tried to kill hated police commissioner of calcutta but due to some miscalculation they killed two british ladies. Out of guilt Prafulla chaki shot himself dead and later Khudiram Bose was arrested and executed.
      • British authority issued shoot at sight warrant against revolutionaries which led to arrest of large number of revolutionaries like Brindra Ghosh, Bhupendra nath Datta, Ullaskar Dutta etc. 
      • These revolutionaries were handed death punishment but due to intervention of chittranjan das. Death punishment was converted into life imprisonment at cellular jail in Andaman.
      • This case was Faught in Alipore Calcutta therefore c/as Alipore conspiracy case.
      • Aurobindo was also framed in this case but he was successfully defended by chittranjan das. After getting exonerated from judicial process Aurobindo decided quit active politics and he embarked on spiritual journey towards Pondicherry.

Delhi:

  • Rash Bihari Bose and Sachindranath Sanyal:
    • In 1912, They decided to kill the viceroy of India Hardinge.
    • When viceroy was taking elephant ride he was attack but it did not led to his killing. Viceroy was critically injured and this led to framing of Delhi Conspiracy Case.
    • It further led to arrest of 4 revolutionaries Awadh Bihari, Amir Chand, Blamukund, and Basanth who were executed and Sachindranath Sanyal was sent to cellular Jail Andaman.

Madras:

  • Vanchi Iyer:
    • He killed hated Tax collector of Tirunelveli {Robert Ashe}

Bombay:

  • Abhinav Bharat:
    • It was created by two brothers Vinayak Damodar Savarkar and Ganesh Damodar Savarkar in 1904. 
    • Earlier in 1899, VS created “Nasik Mitra Mela” through he promoted nationalism. 
    • In 1906 he got opportunity to study law from England. 
    • In Britain, he revolutionary group of India House and wrote a text “Mazzini Charitra” based on life of Italian revolutionary Mazzini. Within the first month, more than 2000 copies of this book were sold.
    • His popularity started to increase in Britain. OTOH in India, members of Abhinav Bharat, Anant Lakshaman Kanhare and Vinayak Deshpande assassinated Nasik Magistrate AMT Jackson.
    • Meanwhile in Britain, one of the revolutionary from India House assassinated Curzon Wylie in London in 1909.
    • All these incidents led to arrest of revolutionaries in Britain and India. Majority of them were deported to cellular jail in Andaman. Which also included deportation of VDS for 2 life term.

Outside India:

  • Bharat Swadeshi Swashasan Samiti India House:
    • It was created by Shayamji Krishna Verma in London. His organization supported indian students who were studying in Britain.
    • He also provided residential accommodation to the indian students and inspired them from nationalist ideas.
    • He also released a revolutionary magazine “Indian sociologist”.
    • VDS became part of India house during his stay in Britain.
  • Madam Bhikaji Cama:
    • She was a rich Parsi lady married to Rustam Cama, who was a pro british lawyer and wanted to enter into politics with the british support.
    • She was a nationalist and during the spread of Bubonic plague in Bombay, she joined the team of Grant Medical college to care for the afflicted. but she also got afflicted with plague.
    • For better recovery she was sent to Britain. After her recuperation initially she came in contact with DBN and later with Shayamji Krishna Verma.
    • She became an active member of India house when british authority were pondering over to deport her to India, she shifted to Paris where she created Paris indian society with the help of SR Rana and MB Godrej in 1906.
    • She happened to be the 1st indian who designed the indian flag. This flag has 3 color strip i.e. Green, Yellow, and Saffron. On the green part 8 lotus flowers were created which represented 8 british indian provinces. In middle strip in Devanagari script “Vande Mataram” was written and in the lower strip image of sun and moon represented indian secularism. for the 1st time this flag was unfurled at International Socialist conference at Stuttgart 1907.
  • Indian Nationalism activity in North America:
    • Reasons:
      1. A large number of indian who were living in north America felt there is more freedom and liberty in NA as compared to India.
      2. They came in touch with the American values of liberty, equality, rationality which was enshrined in the American Constitution.
      3. British colonizers present in NA racially differentiated with indians.
      4. Indians also got contaminated with communist ideas and ideologies in America.
      5. There was enhanced freedom to create organization and release Newspapers and magazines. As a result following activities were happened.
    • Swadesh Sevak at Vancouver by GD Kumar
    • United India House at Seattle By Taraknath and GD Kumar.
      • Taraknath released a magazine called “Free Hindustan”
    • Ramnath Puri released a circular k/as “Circular-i-Azadi”
    • The most important organization was “Hindi Association of Portland”
  • Hindi Association of Portland
    • the 1st meeting of Hindi association was held at house of Kanshiram and it was attended by Bhai Parmanand, Soham Singh Bhakna, Harnam Singh Tundilat, Lala Hardayal.
    • In this meeting they decided to create a “Jugantar Aashram” at San Francisco and further decided to release a bi-weekly newspaper “The Ghadar”.
    • Lala Haryadal was the editor of this newspaper. In one of the issue he re-released Yugantar circular appreciating attack on Viceroy Hardinge.
    • In another edition he wrote a very famous and sensational article “Angrezi Raaj ka Kachha Chhitha” in which he ripped apart the exploitative phase of british authority and called for an armed rebellion against british authority.
  • The Ghadar Movement
    • Immediate factor: Komagata Maru incident
      • In 1913, british authority allowed that some indians can visit Canada as such a Japanese ship Komagatamaru was commissioned for the purpose. 
      • Large number of indians boarded the ship and started for Canada. once they reached to coast of Vancouver Canadian authorities did not allowed them to disembark form the ship.
      • after few days they were forced to return back to India. Reaching the coast of Budge-Budge near Calcutta, indians clashed with british officials as such british open fire which led to killing of more than 50 ppl and majority of them were form Punjab.
      • It sparked the initiation of Ghadar Movement form North western India which rapidly engulfed entire north India.
    • Developments during Ghadar movement:
      • in north and north-west it was organized by Rash Bihari Bose and it also led to foundation of Ghadar party, whose president was Sohan Singh Bhakna.
      • They tried to enlist the help of German chancellor Zimmerman to supply weapons to indian revolutionaries.
      • They tried to provoke indian army to revolt against british authority and participate in Ghadar movement.
      • A young boy named Kartar Singh Sarabha was executed by british authorities which led to very high anti-British emotion.
      • Ghadarites maintained that the movement must be completely secular and there is no scope of religious fanaticism and polarization.
      • Despite brave efforts it was completely suppressed by 1915. Though it continued in the other parts of the world e.g. in San Francisco till 1917.
    • Reasons for its failure: There were many flaws in the Ghadar movement which are as follows
      1. their ideology of violent overthrow of british form india w/o having a standing army of their own.
      2. They were dependent on arms and armaments from germany.
      3. They had wishful thinking that indian army will join the revolt.
      4. They overestimated their strength and under estimated the strength of british. 
      5. Initial strategy created by Lala Hardayal failed completely.
      6. They also faced financial problems to continue with the revolt.
    • Significance of the revolt.
      • It united indians who are living in abroad and infused them with the sense of nationalism.
      • One of the significant contribution of this movement was theme of India's freedom form british colonial rule became an important topic of debate and discussion on the international forums.
      • It created a new genre of revolutionaries who are going to play a very important role in the 3rd of 20th century to carry forward legacy of armed struggle against british authority.
      • They promoted socialist, secular, democratic ideals during the course of the movement.
