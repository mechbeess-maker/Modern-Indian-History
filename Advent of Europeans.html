# Introduction#

Adventure in the sea led to establishment of new global order. European kingdoms started to promote sea fairing activity to earn profit from trade with the eastern world. In this endeavor, Portuguese were the first successful people. Portuguese sailor Vasco da Gama was 1st to find direct sea route to India in 1498. For about a century, Portuguese dominated Indian trade but in the beginning of the 17th century other European companies started to arrive in India.
 
Portuguese were followed by Dutch -> English -> Danes -> French
 
Since the arrival of 1st European in 1498, and till the independence of India in 1947 much water has flown under the bridge (much changed), which changed the shape and discourse of Indian history and society. 
 
India got transformed from the status of a feudalistic, conservative and exclusive social system to a capitalistic, progressive and inclusive social system. This change has happened due to self assertion based on introspection and external stimuli of ideas like freedom, liberty and equality etc. As such India got transformed from One mans rule to peoples rule i.e. Monarchy to Democracy.

# Dutch EIC #

{symbol: VoC, 1st multi-national, they first arrived in Indonesia (native spice -> Clove, Nutmeg and Mace)}
 
It was created in 1602 and was the 1st multinational company in the world. They arrived in India in 1605 and created their 1st warehouse in Masulipatnam in 1605 itself. Before coming to India, they already increased their presence in Indonesia and created their settlements in Batam and Batavia(Jakarta). In India, their expansion happened in quick fashion. They created their warehouses at Cochin, Pulicat, Surat, Balasore, Nagapattanam, Chinsura, Qasim Baazar, Dacca, Patna, Bimlipatnam etc.

In 1759, British EIC defeated Dutch EIC in the battle of Bidara/Chinsura/Hooghly. They were thoroughly defeated after which they decided to focus on Indonesian islands rather than fighting with other Europeans in India. Slowly they stated to wind up their operation in India and by 1825 they completely left India.

Later, the charter was extended for an infinite period till further notice. Over the period this company came to be known as British EIC or English EIC. 
The 1st official envoy of company led by Sir William Hawkins visited Mughal Emperer Jahangir's Court. Impressed by William Hawkins he gave him a Mansabdar Position and British were further allowed to create their warehouse at Surat and Masulipatnam. 
 
The very presence of British in Mughal court was not liked by Portuguese which led to fight between the two. When Hawkins did not get any support from the Mughals, he left India. 
 
In 1612, the British envoy led by Thomas best defeated Portuguese in Battle of Swalley. Jahangir was impressed by this.

-> Mansabdari System
  1. It is derived from the word "Mansab" which means "official Position". It was initiated by Akbar.
  2. All the big officers of the empire were generally reffered as "Mansabdar".
  3. This system has two important components:
    1. Zat: It indicated official rank of a mansabdar in hierarchy. The rank of mansabdar was indicated through numerical value which ranged from 500 to 5000 in nominal terms. In exceptional terms 7000 and 11000 ranks were also given. A.T. rank salary was paid to the mansabdar. Those who got cash salary are k/as Naqdi Mansabdar. Those who were given land grant were k/as Jagirdari Mansabdar. There were two types of Jagir i.e. Tankhwah Jagir -> given for temporary period and Watan Jagir -> It was permanent and hereditary.
    2. Sawar: A.T. Rank and salary, a mansabdar has to maintain particular number of soldiers and horses in the service of mughal monarch.

Not only Jagirdari crisis but other reasons were responsible for the decline. These factors can be divided into political, economic, socio-religious, and military nature.
 
  • Political Causes:
    • The expansionist policy of Aurangzeb sapped the vitality of the mugal empire. {very expensive; it was deccan campaign -> Marathwada, Bijapur and Golconda; never was able to beat shivaji} {shivaji was beaten by Jai singh I in the battle of purandar 1665}
    • Aurangzeb did not respected the line of succession and after his death there was a chaos in mughal politics due to no clear line of succession.
    • After the death of Aurangzeb there was heavy political instability. In just 12 years 6 rulers were appointed to the mughal throne. 
    • Majority of the mughal emperors were incapable and some of them did not have character to rule. Many of them indulged in physical pleasure rather than administration. 
    • Mughal Wazirs became very powerful in this duration and there was intense competition among the nobility to get the position of Wazir.
    • A court factionalism{groupism based on ethnic identity} also emerged in the mughal court and the cooperation between different noble groups completely declined
       
  • Economic causes
    • Jagirdari Crisis: At the core of mughal administration was Mansabdari system. The mughal might depended on the strength of its mansabdars. M'ds played a vital role in the expansion of mughal empire so as their own income.
      • Aurangzeb initiated very long drawn expensive wars in Marathwada, Bijapur and Golconda. After establishing his control in these areas, he declared majority of land in royal category and less land was made available for granting purpose. {initially it was 80%->M'ds and 20%->emperor, but in this expedition it was >50% for emperor}
      • Moreover, the jagirs were highly disproportionate, small and unequal in size. It led to intense fight b/n the Mughal Jagirdars to increase their land holding. It is called as Jagirdari crisis in the Mughal court.
      • Many of the mughal jagirdar tried to extract more taxes, which often led to revolt and it further led to erosion of trust in mughal administration. 
         
  • Socio-Religious Causes
    • The religious divisive policy of Aurangzeb played a vital role in decline
    • The forcible religious conversions were highly detested by majority population.
    • Aurangzeb destroyed large number of temples including somnath temple, kashi vishwanath, keshav rai temple, Govind dev Temple of mathura-vrindavan(built by Raja man singh -> Navratna of Akbar's court, Akbar also paid large sum to build this)
    • A'zb banned many festivals like Holi, Muharram(Shia festival, he was of sunni faith), Navroz
    • He reimposed Jiziya Tax, which was abolished by Akbar.
       
  • Military causes of decline
     
    • 18th century India witnessed the consolidation of European powers in India. Though they were not great in numbers but they had highly upgraded military technology. OTOH, there was no change in the mughal army and they were still fighting with old weapon and technology. Once they faced the opponent like british they proved to be highly inferior. 
These kingdoms were emerged after the death of Aurangzeb.
 
Successor States <- <- Mughal Empire -> -> Rebellious States
 
  1. Successor States: These were semi-independent and completely autonomous states. These states were created by the erstwhile nobility of mughals. They did not cutoff their complete links with mughals and they respected the nominal leadership of mughal emperor. They sent annual tributes to mughal monarch. They also continues minting coins in the name of mughal monarch and friday prayers were also raised in his name. 
    • Eg: Bengal {1717}, Awadh {1722}, Hyderabad {1724}
       
  2. Rebellious States: These states were independent kingdoms but during the tenure of Aurangzeb some of their areas were annexed and captured. After the death of Aurangzeb the rulers of these areas re-establlished their complete independence and autonomy. They didn't respected nominal leadership of mughals and they were not supposed to mint coins in the name of mughal monarch. They were also not supposed to sent any tribute to the mughal emperor.
    • Eg: Mysore, Maratha and Punjab
Rajput's belonged to neither of them. They were allied kingdoms since the rule of Akbar, and even in later Mughals we saw steps taken by emperor to have good relationship with them.

Anglo-French Rivalry (Carnatic Wars)#

-> There are 4 main players in this -> B's, French, Nizams, and carnatic
 
Hyderabad
  • Hyderabad was one of the rich province of Mughal India. In between 1713-1722, Chin-Qilich Khan was the Governor of Hyd. In 1722, he became Mughal wazir. He wanted to reform corrupt mughal administration but due to very suspicious nature of mughal monarch. He resigned from the position of wazir. He decided to pursue his own ambition. In 1724, he defeated and killed Mubariz Khan in Battle of kheda and established Asaf Jahi Dynasty in Hyd.
  • His successors came to be known as Nizams of Hyderabad. 
Carnatic
  • Carnatic was the coromandal coast of India and its hinterland. In 1690, Aurangzeb recognised Nawabship of carnatic. Carnatic was kept under the legal purview of Hyderabad Governor. In 1724, when hyderabad became semi-independent autonomous state, automatically carnatic also became free from Mughal control.
  • Carnatic nawab Saadat-allah-khan started to take his own decisions without any interference of Mughal emperor of his nobles.
  • After his death, Dost Ali became the next carnatic nawab.
Background:
  • In 1740, after the death of Dost Ali war of succession started in carnatic. At this time, French governor Dupliex decided to interfere in the political affairs of carnatic so as to get a greater political milage in Carnatic. Very soon, BEIC also followed in the footsteps of french. They started to interfare in Carnatic politics.
  • It led to development in bitter fight between B's and F'h which continued for more than 20 years. These fights are referred as Carnatic Wars.
1st Anglo-French War (1742-46):
  • In 1740, Austrian War of Succession started in Europe after the death of King of Hapsburg Empire Carles Vth. The two contenders for powers were daughter of King charles Maria Theresa and another was Charles Albert. B's supported daughter's claim and french supported Charles Albert which led to war of succession. 
  • Both the companies also started to fight not only in India but also in North America. French Gov Dupliex attacked on Madras. At this time B's sent help message to Nawab of Carnatic Md. Anwaruddin. But both B's and Carnatic forces were thoroughly defeated in Madras by French. 
  • OTOH, in NA, B's captured french area of Nova Scottia and Breron Island.
  • This war ended with Treaty of Aix-La-Chappel.
  • As per the treaty, Madras was restored to the b's and F'h got back their territories in NA.
2nd A-F War/Carnatic War (1749-1754)
  • In 1749, French defeated and killed Nawab of Carnatic Anwaruddin in Battle of Ambur. It led to war of succession b/n Md. Ali(B) and Chanda Sahib(F)
  • In Hyderabad, death of Asaf Jah happened in 1748, which led to war of succession b/n Nasir Jung(B) and Muzaffar Jung (F)
  • Finally, French were victorious in installing their candidates at both places. Initially the B's attitude was very callous and they accepted their defeat but soon after the appointment of new General Saunder's they felt to to put up some resistance against french.
  • At this point of time a young clerk Robert Clive volunteered to fight and he also suggested alternative strategy to defeat french. Saunders gave a chance to Robert Clive.
    • Seige of Arcot: With a small section of British force RC entered into carnatic capital arcot when, CS was marching towards Trichonapally(Trichy) {to kill Md.A}. In turn of events, Chanda sahib has to return back to Arcot{When the news of the capture of Arcot reached Chanda Saheb} but in his own capital he was besieged by B forces and finally defeated and killed. This was the 1st victory of B against Carnatic Nawab and French. The war ended with the treaty of Pondicherry in 1754, as per the treaty French were forced to recall Dupleix from India, and the French were allowed to retain their influence on Hyderabad for time being.
3rd A-F Battle/carnatic War (1756-1763)
  • In 1756, a seven-year war broke out in Europe which renewed the Anglo-French rivalry all across the world to establish their imperial dominance (Hegemony).
  • In India, the most important and decisive battle was fought in 1760 known as the battle of Wandiwash.
  • In this battle, British officer Sir Eyre Coote thoroughly defeated French general Count de Lally. and it ended with treaty of paris (1763)
    • Treaty of Paris: 
      • As per the treaty, French were allowed to retain all their territories in India created prior to 1749.
      • Further allowed to continue trade and commerce in India but they had to accept the British Protectorate in India.
      • It winded up the imperialist ambition in India to create a colonial empire in India and B's emerged as sole colonial power in India.
Factors responsible for French defeat
  • French was a Government-owned company which made it slow in terms of decision making. On the other hand, British was a private company and decision-making was fast.
  • French faced financial difficulties because it needed many approvals from French authorities. On the other hand, the British did not face any financial difficulty. After the Plassey plunder, ready finances were available at the expanse of British.
  • English east India company had a much superior naval force by 1760. Moreover, they operated from Bombay and Calcutta while the French operated only from Pondicherry.
  • The recall of Dupleix did not prove to be beneficial for the French and there was a dearth of people expert in strategy-making, on the other hand, the British got Robert Clive the master strategist.
  • The French general Count-De-Lally was arrogant and rash towards his own people because of his ill-temper there was no cohesion in the French army. While the British were operating under a unified command of Coote.
