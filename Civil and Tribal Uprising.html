Civil and Tribal Uprising

# Features of the tribal society#

  • Tribal society are homogeneous in nature, which means they all belong to the same genetic code or they all are blood related.
  • Tribal society's were unstratified in nature. which means there were no division in their society.
  • They generally inhabited geographically isolated areas and they never liked interference of outsiders in their area.
  • They practice unique culture whereby nature was given very high importance.
  • In some of the tribal society's sacred Groves were highly revered whereby they protected some elements of nature.
  • There was balance sexual division of labor in society's.
  • They had distinct means of livelihood which was dependent on nature.
  • Tribal practiced common property holding pattern and they didn't practice the concept of pvt property.
     

# Eastern India & Bengal#

  1. Sanyasi Uprising (1770):
    • In bengal large number of Naga and Giri Sanyasi's were present. They were known for visiting holy religious places and they often carried religious weapons with them.
    • In 1770, b's imposed restriction on their movement and they were not allowed to visit any religious place. In this situation they started attacking british factories and Treasuries.
       
  2. Fakir Uprising (1776):
    • Fakirs were wandering Muslim religious mendicants. They also provided religious services by visiting one village to another. They also carried weapons while travelling.
    • British authorities imposed restrictions on their movement also. 
    • ÔåÆ In this situation Fakir and Sanyasi of Bengal started to collude with each other and combinedly attacked on British treasuries.
    • Chiraj Ali, Bhawani Pathak, and Devi Chaudharani were the notable supporters of Sanyasi & Fakir Uprising.
    • Later a novel Anandmath was written by Bankim Chandra Chatterjee, which was based on the background of these revolts. One of the unique aspect of this novel was the song "Vande Mataram". Which later became song of the freedom struggle.
       
  3. Pagal Panthi Uprising (1830):
    • It was a semi religious sect founded in north bengal by Karam Shah. Later in 1830, his son Tipu took up the cause of peasants against exploitation by zamindars and british agents. 
    • He advised peasants to withhold the revenue and not submit before the exploitative people.
       
  4. Khasi Uprising (1829):
    • British were creating a road to connect Brahmaputra valley with Sylhet. 
    • For this purpose large number of labors from plain areas were brougt but later after the completion of road, B's tried to habitat the outsiders in this area. In this situation Khasis organized the revolt under the leadership of Tirath Singh.
    • Very soon this revolt was joined by the other Tribal communities like Garo, Khampti's and Singhpo's.
       
  5. Kol Mutiny (1831):
    • Kol were the tribal of Ranchi, Singhbhum, Hazaribagh and Palamu. It is mineral belt of India and Britishers tried to control this area through overt and covert measures.
    • they started transferring land from Kol headman to outsiders from bengal and other plain areas.
    • In this scenario, Kol organized revolt under the leadership of Buddhu Bhagat. They tried to burn property papers of transfer. B's suppressed this revolt with immense brutality, which led to killing of >1000 Kol People.
       
  6. Paika Rebellion (1817):
    • Paikas were the landed militia of the kingdom of Khurdha(OD). They provided military support to Khurdha king and in return they were granted rent free states.
    • In 1803, B's started to tinker with the land revenue system of Odisha, which often led to peasant uprising.
    • In 1817, B's revoked rent free estates of Paika's and now they were supposed to make payments. In this situation, Paika's organized revolt under the leadership of Bakshi Jaganandhu. 
    • Initially they were successful of expelling british forces form khurdha. Later B's came with greater prep and suppressed the revolt.
       
  7. Ahom Rebellion ():
    • During the 1st Burma War (1824-26), B's used upper Assam area or Ahom territory. They pledged that after the end of war they will withdraw form Ahom territory but it was a false promise.
    • In this situation, ahoms organized revolt under Gomdhar Konwar. It was a successful rebellion which led to handing over Ahom territory to their Raja Purandhar Singh Narendra.
       
  8. Khond Uprising (1838-56):
    • Khond's were the primitive tribes of Niamgiri Hills of Odisha. They were known for their inhumane practice of Human sacrifice once in a while {Mariah}.
    • B's tried to stop this initially through persuasion but later through force, when a rebellion was organized by Chakra Bisoi.
       
  9. Fairazi Revolt (1837-56):
     
    • It was a semi religious sect in Islam founded by Haji Shariat-ullah. His son Dadu Mian, took up the cause of peasants and asked them to withhold the revenue and do not pay any taxes to the B's and Zamindars.
       
    • He gave a statement "All the land belongs to the god, and no man has the right to levy taxes on it"
       
  10. Santhal Revolt** (1855-56):
     
    • It was the most massive of all the tribal revolts. In santhali language it is called as "Santhal Hool".
       
    • Santhals were the tribes of the area b/n Bhagalpur and Rajmahal, which they called Daman-i-Koh. They cleared the dense forest of the foot hills of rajmahal and made it agriculturally cultivable.
       
    • B's brought santhal area under umbrella of many taxes and also started to bring large number of outsiders particularly form bengal in this area. It led to livelihood crisis and also destruction of unique culture of santhals.
       
    • Very soon santhals figured out that B's were behind it and they were hand in glove with outsiders(Santhali -> Diku).
       
    • In this situation two brothers, Sidu Murmu and Kanhu Murmu emerged as their leaders. They maintained that Thakur has communicated to them to take up arms against diku's and it will bring satyug.
       
    • But unfortunately this uprising was suppressed with immense brutality.
       
    • B's claimed killing of 15k santhals but in reality more than 25k people were killed.
       
    • One of the impact of this revolt is that in later times British declared Santhal area as a separate paragna with more autonomy given to santhal community in their administration.
       
    • Charles Dickens on Santhal people - He was highly impressed by their sense of battlefield ethics, going so far as to contrast their honor favorably against European armies of the time.
       
    • "There seems also to be a sentiment of honour among them; for it is said that they use poisoned arrows in hunting, but never against their foes. If this be the case and we hear nothing of the poisoned arrows in the recent conflicts, they are infinitely more respectable than our civilized enemy, the Russians, who would most likely consider such forbearance as foolish, and declare that is not war."
       
  11. Munda Revolt (1899-1900):
     
    • This movement was initiated by Birsa Munda. Mundas were the tribal people of Jharkhand particularly Khunti, Tamar, Bandagaon, and Ranchi.
       
    • Their main resentment was against British authorities and outsiders who were involved in land grabbing. Initially Birsa Munda tried to fight it through the route of Judiciary, seeing no results through the process, he decided to organize a big revolt against the exploitative system of British rule.
       
    • He envisaged a egalitarian society which was free from any kind of exploitation, internal or external.
       
    • During the course of the revolt he was arrested and tried in court but unfortunately due to poisoning in jail his death happened. 
       
# North India
 
  1. Wahabi movement (1840s):
     
    • It was religious revivalist movement in Islam based on the teachings of Abdul Wahab of Saudi Arabia.
       
    • Initially they were against the Sikh rule of Maharaja Ranjit Singh but after annexation of Punjab by B's, they turned against british and in 1857 revolt they participated in large numbers.
       
  2. Namdahari or Kuka Revolt
     
    • It was a religious purification campaign in Sikhism initiated by Bhagat Jawahar Mal in 1840.
       
    • But later after the annexation of Punjab they took up arms against british authority and participated in large numbers in 1857 revolt.
       
# West India
 
  1. Bhil Uprising (1822 - 1846):
     
    • Bhils were the tribals of Khadesh area of Western Ghats. They were not reconciled to british rule in their area. Further british occupation to their area brought agricultural hardships, employment hardships, restriction on forest movement and burden of taxes.
       
    • Initially revolt was organized under the leadership of Seva ram
       
    • By the end of 1846 there was agreement with B's whereby B's allowed access to forest produce to Bhil community.
       
  2. Koli Uprising (1829-1844):
     
    • They were the fisherman community of western ghats. Earlier they had good source of living through fishing activity.
       
    • But B's brought livelihood crisis for them and also destroyed the coastal forts of Koli's.
       
  3. Ramosi Uprising (1822, 1825, 1835):
     
    • They were the hill tribes of western ghats and they mainly resided in higher altitudes. They had complete control on the vegetation and produce of higher altitudes. British authorizes imposed restriction on their usage of forest produce.
       
    • Initially they revolted under the leadership of Chittur Singh but later in 1838 when British removed their Raja Pratap Singh of Satara, the entire community took up arms against British authority.
       
  4. Gadkari Revolt of Kolhapur and Sawantwadi (1844):
     
    • Gadkari's were the traditional military class of Maratha empire. They protected the Maratha force. But after British occupation of this area, gadkari unit was disbanded facing employment crisis initially they revolted form Kolhapur and later form Sawantwadi.
       
# South India
 
  1. Revolt of Raja of Vizianagram (1783-94) -> Northern Circar area:
     
    • In northern Circar area of Andhara, B's demanded 3 lakh rupees for the recognition of his rule of Raja of Vizianagram. He refused which led to b.o.Padmanabham. But unfortunately he was killed and later his son reconciled with B's by paying 5 lakh rupees.
       
  2. Revolt of Dewan Velu Thampi (1805) -> Travancore in Kerela:
     
    • Velu Thampi was the dewan of Travancore kingdom. Travancore was put under subsidiary alliance system and after some time they realized it is difficult to rule due to high handedness of B's. He was assisted by Nair community of Kerela.
       
    • But unfortunately velu thampi was captured and hanged publicly to instil fear among people.
       
  3. Rampa Rebellion (1879) -> exactly same as Ramosi:
     
    • Rampa's were the hill tribesman of eastern ghats and they inhabited higher altitude of hill ranges. British authority imposed restriction on their forest movement and use of forest produce.
       
    • Facing livelihood crisis, they revolted which led to the recognition of their forest rights.
