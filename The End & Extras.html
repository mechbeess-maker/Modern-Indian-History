The End & Extras...

  • QIM led to violence form the side of state so as the masses.
  • At this time B's asked Gandhi to condemn the violence of masses. Gandhi not only refused but went on to say it is Leonine violence of state which have provoked the masses, if they are serious about stopping violence then they must stop their own violence.
  • Gandhi announced 21 day fast against state violence. In the course of fast Gandhi's health started to deteriorate. On one hand anxious nation started to plead for Gandhi's health on the other hand, british authority went ahead with funeral preparation of Gandhi.
  • At this time 3 members of viceroy executive council, MS Aney, NR Sarkar, HP Mody, resigned from viceroy executive council. They said they were party to QIM suppression but they would be not party to Gandhi's death.
  • Parallel governments:
    1. Balliya District of UP by Chity Pandey in Aug 1942: It survived for just 1 month and in this duration they tried to create some departments of administration like health, education etc.
    2. Jatiya Sarkar Tamluk Midnapore : It was formed in Dec 1942 and it survived for more then a year. They also created many department of administration like health, education, agriculture. They created an armed force k/as Vidyut Vahini, which performed relief work during tropical cyclone in bengal.
    3. Prati Sarkar of Satara, MH: It was formed in mid 1943 by two leaders Nana Patil and YB Chaven. It was the longest surviving parallel government. They not only created departments of administration but also Nyaydan Mandal to dispense justice. They also conducted Gandhi marriages (mass marriages with crowd funding)

CR Formula (1944):

  • In 1944, Rajagopalachari proposed a formula to break the deadlock b/n congress and ML which is as follows:
    • for the time being ML must abandon demand of separate nation Pakistan and it must cooperate with Indian national congress in seeking independence of India.
    • After the end of the war a plebiscite shall be held in the areas where Muslims are in majority NW and east to decide whether or not a separate nation would be created.
    • In case of partition, agreements would be made on Finance, defense and all the essential matters.
  • The above terms shall be binding. Only in case of complete transfer of power by british authority.
  • MAJ did not agreed to the said plebiscite provision as such it ended in no result.

Desai-Liaquat Pact (1944):

  • Bholabhai Desai and Liaquat Ali khan of ML met to break the deadlock b/n congress and ML. They agreed that ML will abandon the demand of Pakistan but in return they would be given parity in council of ministers and in the interim government.
  • Moreover, they also agreed that 20% seats would be reserved for depressed classes.

# Shimla Conference and Wavell's Offer:#

  • General elections were about to happen in Britain by the end of 1945. Moreover anti-incumbency factors were very strong in Britain and it was almost sure that conservative party of churchill will be replaced by labor party.
  • Viceroy Wavell was instructed to convene the meeting of major indian leaders to arrive at constitutional solution.
  • All the major leaders of congress including Gandhi were released form Jail and invited to participate in Shimla conference where viceroy Wavell proposed his solution:

Wavell's Offer:

  1. All the members of viceroy ex. council will be indians governor general and commander in chief of forces.
  2. Creation of an interim govt within the premise of GoI Act 1935.
  3. In council of ministers, parity would be given to H's and M's.
  4. No constitutional development will happen against the wishes of minority.
  5. After the war is over discussion on new constitution will happen. 
  6. viceroy will enjoy veto power on the aid and advise of ministers on important matters.

# Azad Hind Fauz & INA:#

  • The original Idea of INA was proposed by Rash Bihari Bose. 
  • He negotiated with Japanese leaders to give prisoners of war of indian origin to create INA and later INA would be guided by Subash Chandra Bose.
  • By Sept 1942, idea of INA came into reality due to the efforts of Capt. Mohan Singh in Singapore.
  • In early 1943, Bose arrived in SE Asia and he took complete control of INA.
  • Bose created temporary GoI in exile.
  • He also crated Azad Hind Bank and Azad Hind Radio.
  • In July 1944, Bose addressed the nation through Azad hind radio, he seeked blessings of Gandhi and called him вҖңFather of nationвҖқ
  • Bose created different regiments of INA, named after the major nationalist of india like gandhi, Nehru, Maulana Azad and regiment named after himself and a women regiment named rani Laxmi bai.
  • Bose also gave a slogan вҖңTum mujhe khoon do, mai thume Azadi DungaвҖқ вҶ’ вҖңIf you do desire freedom be prepared to wade through your bloodвҖқ
  • Bose started the dreaded campaign of INA i.e. Imphal and Kohima campaign in NE India with the help of southern branch of Japanese army but unfortunately his campaign ended in disaster.
  • INA was outnumbered in 1/10 ratio by british indian forces. Moreover Japan surrendered unconditionally after nuclear bombardment in Hiroshima and Nagasaki.
  • As such INA also surrendered in Singapore. Large number of INA soldiers/officers were brought in India for trial.
  • OTOH, Bose died in a mysterious plane crash at Taiwan capital Taipei.

INA Trials:

  • INA officers were brought to India for open court trials. At this time congress created INA defence committee with the leaders like JLN, Bholabhai Desai, TB Sapru, KN Katju, Lt. Col. Horilal, Asaf Ali.
  • During Delhi trials 3 INA officers were given punishment who were Prem Sehegal, Gurabaksha Dillon, and Shahnawaz Hussain. This punishment led to huge protest and movement in the different parts of country.
  • Meanwhile during the trials 3 upsurges happened:
    1. Nov 1945, during Calcutta Trial at Dalhousie Chowk
    2. 11th Feb 1946 during trials at Deshpriya Park Calcutta when INA officer Rashid Ali was given 7 years punishment
    3. 18th Feb 1946 in Royal Indian Navy of Bombay.

RIN Revolt:

  • On 18th Feb 1946, 1100 Indian ratings of HMIS-Talwar announced strike against racial discrimination and unpalatable food was served for many days. 
  • Moreover one of the rating BC Dutta shouted britishers quit India. After which he was thoroughly mistreated.
  • Revolt of HMIS Talwar further reached to all the other ships docked at Gateway of India and it further inspired revolt of Calcutta and royal indian navy Karachi.
  • This episode is regarded as last nail in the coffin of british rule in India

# Independence with Partition:#

On 19th Feb 1946, British PM Clement Atlee announced that he is going to send cabinet mission to India with 3 officers i.e. Stafford Cripps, AV Alexander and Pethick Lawrence.

Cabinet Mission (1946):

  1. It proposed the creation of interim govt for transitional period and creation of constituent assembly of India to frame constitution fo India.
  2. On paper it rejected creation of new nation Pakistan.
  3. It proposed creation of strong federation and a weak center. 
  4. It divided stated into 3 groups
    1. Group A: Hindu majority states like UP, CP, Bihar, Odisha, Bombay and madras. it will also include commissionary provice of Delhi, Ajmer and coorg.
    2. Group B: Muslim majority states in NW which includes Punjab, nwfp, and Sindh. It will also include commissionry province of Baluchistan.
    3. Group C: Muslim majority states in east i.e. Assam and Bengal
  5. Congress inquired about nature of groupings i.e. compulsory or optional. to this B's replied it is compulsory. As a result congress said that they will change this provision with their majority in constituent assembly. At this time Muslim League gave a call for Direct Action Day on 16th Aug 1946.
    • It was an unfateful day which led to killing of more than killing of >5000 people in Calcutta itself.

On 2nd of Sept, Interim government was created under PM JLN. ML boycotted to join the government but later in Oct 1946, viceroy Wavell brought 5 members of ML to joint the govt who were Liaquat Ali Khan, Gajanafar Khan, Rashid Ali Ahmad, Jogendranath Mondal, II Chundrigar.

But the members of ML rather than cooperation created obstruction in interim govt.

On 9th Dec 1946, Constituent assembly was created to frame the constitution of India. In Constituent assembly also ML created hinderances.

In Feb 1947, once again british PM Clement Atlee announced that british will start transfer of power by June 1948. But in India instances of communal violence increased as such in march 1947 Lord Louis Mountbatten was sent as last Viceroy of India.

Mountbatten was given power to decide the matter then and there. Initially he came up with Dickie Bird Plan aka Plan Balkan. As per this plan all the indian provinces and princely states were to be given successor state status. JLN heavily opposed this plan and said it will lead to balkanization of India. Immediately this plan was scrapped.

3rd June Plan / Mountbatten Plan:

  • It proposed the creation of two dominion i.e. India and Pakistan.
  • Mountbatten also decided the newly emerging Pakistan will also be partitioned so as to keep maximum unity with indian union.
  • As per this plan Hindu part of bengal will join India and so as Hindu and Sikh part of Punjab will also join India.
  • He also created boundary commission under the chairmanship of sir Radcliff.
  • 3rd June plan of Mountbatten was introduced as India independence bill in british parliament which got passed on 18th July 1947.
  • As per this act India's freedom day was 15th Aug and Pakistan's 14th Aug.
  • Boundary commission report was already available by 10th Aug but it was made public after independence so that responsibility of communal killing will not fall on british heads.

# Development of Education:#

  • Right from the beginning B's were of the view that there is a need to educate some indians who are brown in colour but british in behavior.
  • In 1781, Warren Hastings created Calcutta madrasa to impart traditional Islamic education. 
  • In 1791, Jonathan Duncan created Sanskrit college at Banaras to impart traditional Sanskrit medium education.
  • By 1800, Wellesley created Civil Servant Training College at Fort William Calcutta. But later it was disapproved by court of directors and 1806 they created a separate civil servant college at Haileybury in England.
  • Charter Act 1813, allocated 1 lakh rupees for the promotion of education in India. But it led to debate and discussion regarding mode of education. They got divided into two groups:
    • Anglicist вҶ’ who favored English medium education
    • Orientalist вҶ’ who favored vernacular medium education
  • No money was released till 1820. After 1820s little bit of money is released as per instrument of instruction.
  • In 1833, GG of India William Bentick appointed Macauley committee to resolve this issue. He favored Anglicist view. He also recommended official language of India {Persian} must be replaced by English.

Downward Filtration Theory (Trickle Down Effect):

  • William Bentick was of the view that 1L rupees is a meager amount to educate all indians.
  • He said with this allocated money only few indians can be educated. In turn it would be responsibility of indians to educate the masses.
  • In this way education will trickle down to indians in a gradual way.

Woods Dispatch (1854):

  • SoS Charles Wood gave his famous dispatch on education in India in 1854.
  • It rejected downward filtration theory and maintained that it is responsibility of british authority to educate indians.
  • The provisions were very progressive and it started to be regarded as mangna carta of education in India.
  • Provisions:
    1. It created 3 levels of educaiton along with different meduum of instruction.
      1. Primary educaion in vernacular medium.
      2. Secondary education in Anglo-vernacular medium 
      3. Higher education in English medium through established universities in presidency towns. As a result three universities were created i.e. Bombay, madras and Calcutta
    2. It called for teachers training, vocational education, and womens education.
    3. It further emphasized on establishment of more colleges of engineering and technology so as new Agricultural research institutes (Thomson college of Civil Engineering, Roorkee 1847 was the 1st engineering college of India and Agricultural research institute at Pusa was the 1st agricultural research institute estd in April 1905, originally its name was imperial agricultural institute)
    4. It said education in public institute must be secular in outlook.

Hunter Commission of 1882:

  • It supported continuation of primary education in vernacular. But it prescribed secondary education must be diversified into two types i.e. vocational and literary education

Thomas Raleigh Committee:

  • It was constituted by Viceroy Curzon in 1902.
  • On the recommendation of this committee, the Indian Universities Act was passed in 1904 to provide a regulatory framework for Indian universities.

Sadler Commission 1916:

  • This committee was appointed to review the functioning of Calcutta University and make recommendations for its better functioning.
  • But, its recommendations proved to be equally beneficial to the entire educational sector of India.
  • For the first time, two Indians were part of this committee, i.e. Ashutosh Mukherjee, and Zia-Uddin-Ahmad. (Later Ashutosh Mukherjee became the Vice-Chancellor of Calcutta University.)
  • Recommendations (very progressive in nature вҶ’ tf adopted all indian educational scenario):
    • It suggested bringing efficiency in secondary education for better functioning of the universities.
    • It proposed 12 years of schooling and a 3-year degree course.
    • It maintained that the boards for High School and Intermediate education must be separated.
    • It further proposed the establishment of more new universities to cater educational needs of indians. As a result, in between 1916 to 1921, seven new universities were created which are:
      • 1. BHU (Banaras Hindu University) вҶ’ 1916
      • 2. Aligarh
      • 3. Lucknow
      • 4. Patna
      • 5. Dacca
      • 6. Mysore
      • 7. Hyderabad (Osmania University)

Hartog Commission 1929:

  • It suggested bringing efficiency in education through already established educational institutes.
  • It forbade the creation of new educational institutions in India. 

Wardha Scheme of Education (1937):

  • It was proposed by Gandhi and the elaborate syllabus of Wardha education was created by Zakir Hussain.
  • It was also known as вҖңNai Taleem.вҖқ
  • It promoted common basic minimum education to all indians and also the promotion of values of ethics and morality.
  • Gandhi promoted traditional hand on learning and this scheme was implemented by many of the congress provincial governments.

Sargeant Plan of Education (1944):

  • It was the last proposal of the British before Independence.
  • It wanted to bring universal basic education to Indians but unfortunately, it did not take off.

S Radhakrishnan Committee 1948:

  • The committee was constituted to suggest measures for Indian universities.
  • As per the recommendations of this committee, University Grants Commission was created to provide a regulatory framework and financial assistance to Indian universities.
  • Later in 1956, UGC Act was passed and it became a statutory autonomous body. 
  • UGC decides the pattern of funding and regulation of indian universities.
