Salient aspects of Bengal:
  • It was the richest province of Mughal India and became further rich in tenure of MQK due to his effective leadership.
    • Bengal had a very effective revenue collection system. MQK preferred few powerful zamindars instead of large number of smaller zamindars for revenue collection. By the time of his death in 1727, 15 powerful zamindars of Bengal were responsible for >60% revenue collection of Bengal.
    • In Bengal there was growing importance of merchant-bankers. They proved as a supportive pillar at each and every level of transaction. They provided cheap loan to the people which further led to build of trust in society. 
    • Bengal was unique in the sense that its most important nobility did not came from jagirdari but from merchant banker family. The most important of them were the Jagat Seths.
      • In 1717, Merchant-banker Manickchand supported MQK against mughal emperor Farukhsiyar. As a. token of gratitude, manikchand was given minting rights of bengal. His son fatehchand emerged as the richest person in the contemporary world.
      • Mughal emperor, Rangeela gave him the title of Jagat Seths.
      • Jagat seths build a very trustworthy relationship in Bengal society, as they provided loans of cheaper interest rates. They released Hundi's (Bill of exchange) for higher amount loans which started from 1 lakh rupees till 2 crore rupees. 
      • It was popular in Bengal that if Jagat seths collapse, trade in Bengal will also collapse.
    • In 1740s there was a political turmoil in north and northwest Indian due to the invasions of Nadir Shah and Abdaili. At this time oceanic trade stated to thrive in Bengal with renewed interest of Europeans.
    • During the Tenure of MQK many progressive initiatives were taken regarding Agriculture and Manufactring. Bengal emerged as the manufacturing hub of the world. Eg: Dakha -> Textile production hub of India.
    • Bengal had very negligent import demand but it had very high volume of export of manufactured goods which resulted into favorable Balance of Trade with Bengal.
    • The trade in bengal led to huge accumulation of Bullion particularly gold. 
    • Trade in bengal was win win situation for every stakeholder eg: Producers->Merchant-Bankers->Europenan traders and rulers. Moreover, European companies needed permission of the Indian rulers to operate in India. To get this they often made Peshkash(nazrana) to Indian rulers.

> Regional Power: Mysore
 
Mysore was the vice-royality of vijaynagar kingdom. After Battle of Talikota (1565), decline of vijaynagar started. At this point of time Mysore emerged as an independent territory carved out form vijaynagar. During the regime of Aurangzeb some of the areas of Mysore came under Mughal captivity. Later after death of him, mysore restored its complete Independence under Wodeyar rulers.
 
Krishnaraja Wodeyar was the ruler in the initial decade of 18th cen. By 1730s two of his ministers i.e. Nanjraja and Devaraja usurped the power from krishnaraja Wodeyar and started to rule. 
 
Haider Ali (

Due to his effective functioning he rose to the position of commander in Cheif of Mysore forces. In 1761, he threw Nanjaraja and devaraja from power and installed himself as the ruler of Mysore. 
     
Salient features of Mysore:
 
  1. With the help of french officials, Hyder Ali created a modern arsenal(Weapon making factory) at Dindigul.
  2. He also introduced western methods of training in his army {similar to the French model}
     
He organized his army under Risala System, whereby army was divided into many risalas or units. Each of the Risala had equal number of soldiers, horses, weapons and a commander directly appointed by Hyder Ali. There was clear chain of command starting from the ruler and going up to the soldiers.
 
  1. Mysore had very effective revenue policy:
    • It followed the mughal model whereby land was Surveryed and classified a.t. its fertility.
    • revenue was collected by salaried officials and it was collected only in cash.
    • very often mysore fiscal policy/revenue policy is considered as Military fiscalism (>50% of revenue was allocated to army)
    • Both Hyder ali and tipu sultan promoted agricultural Manufactring whereby sericulture and textile was promoted. They also repaired the old irrigation channels and also created new ones.
    • They also sent many trade missions to countries like Afghanistan, Iran, turkey and France.
       
Reasons which led to fight of Mysore with its neighbors:
 
  • In a very short span of time there was rapid territorial expansion of Mysore under Hyder Ali and Tipu Sultan. Boundaries of Mysore stretched from Krishna River in north and malabar in south west. Initially, Hyder Ali did not respected the Bufferzones and unilaterally tried to annex the Buffer zone territories held by Polygars and deshmukhs. 
     
  • This brought Mysore in confrontation with its neighbors.
     
  • The Francophil policy of Mysore (brotherly love) was highly detested by English.
     
  • In 1785, Tipu Sultan came up with an instruction whereby he banned trade of black pepper and cardamom from the ports in his territory. In 1788, he explicitly banned any kind of trade with BEIC. Company was offended by it.
  • Both HA and TS wanted to create a big Islamic kingdom of south India with a desire to control Maratha politics also.

It was only in the areas of Gujarat, Malwa and Khandesh, where they kept some system of administration but not in the areas of Punjab and Odisha. As such areas without administration were prone to attack by the others.

  • Abdaili brought Swivel guns in this battle which were operated from camels.

  • Maratha forces were thoroughly defeated and also led to death of Sadashiv Rao Bhau,  Vishwash Rao, and Shamsher Bahadur.

Subsidiary Alliance System:
It was formulated by GG of bengal Wellesely to expand b's territory in India and to estd. British paramountey. {B's would be supreme power in India and Indian powers would be subordinate to B's}
  • The native power entering into alliance will have to accept b's forces in their territory and they will also have to pay the maintenance cost of British army. 
  • If they failed to make the payments on time, in this situation some part of their territory will be chipped off as equivalent to payment. 
  • Native power cannot enter in alliance with anybody w/o the permission of B's.
  • They cannot declare war against any other power without the permission of B's.
  • They cannot employ any European other than British in their court.
  • A British resident will be posted in the native court who will look after terms and conditions of alliance (but the British resident often Interfere in internal politics)
  • In return British promised that they will protect and safeguard native territory form any external or internal danger. {The promise which was seldom observed}

> Regional Power: Modern Sikh state of Punjab
 
The land of Punjab was highly fertile area and also birthplace of non-sectrian religion Sikhism. Starting from Guru Nanak and by the time of Guru Gobind, Sikhism became the most popular and accepted religion of punjab. After the death of last guru in 1707, Banda Bahadur was the 1st political appointee of Punjab. After execution of Banda BAhadur fragmentation of Punjab started to happen. It led to creation of 13 confederacy know as Misl.
 
Out of these 13 confederacy -> 12 Sikh and 1 Muslim
 
By 1790's Sukerchakiya was the most powerful misl under the leadership of Ranjeet Singh. He tried to bring all the Sikh misl's together and by 1799 he was completely successful in the creation of modern Sikh state of Punjab.
 


 
Maharaja Ranjeet Singh went on to create 2nd strongest army of Asia with the help of British officers like Ventura, Allard, Court and Gardner. He also introduced western methods of army training. He established very cordial relationship with B's. 
 
In 1809, Maharaja decided to extend his territories in the cic-satluj area. At this time the local chiefs of this area appealed the B's for intervention. As such t.o.Amritsar was signed b/n B's and Ranjeet singh. As per the treaty maharaja recognized cis Satluj as British protectorate and in return B's recognized sovereign authority of maharaja in the trans satluj area.
The terms of treaty were respected till the lifetime of Maharaja. After the death in 1839, there was quick succession of rulers and at this time B's got opportunity to interfare in Punjab politics. 
Within 2 years in quick succession following people were appointed as Maharaja:
Khadak -> Navnihal -> Sher -> Dhuleep singh (minor, Regent: Rani Jindan)
At the time of Dhuleep singh Rani Jindan was exercising power. At this time British intervention in punjab politics increased further. They also tried to create dissension in punjab court. They often provoked Khalsa army.
 
In 1845, on the pretext of building a bridge on satluj they brought huge army. It was a highly provocative act which led to 1st Anglo-sikh war in 1845.
 
In this fight Khalsa army was throughly defeated and it ended with t.o.Lahore. As per the treaty half of the punjab was taken over by B's and size of the khalsa army was also reduced to half. Moreover, Rani Jindan was supposed to pay 1 crore rupees as war damage price to B's. When punjab was unable to make the payment, B's sold kashmir to Gulab singh(Dogra Dynasty) in 75 lakh rupees.
 
British resident of Punjab removed governor of Multan (Mulraj) in 1848. He appointed his own agent in Multan. At this time mulraj revolted and this revolt was joined by Khalsa army. they made a last attempt to throw B's form punjab.
 
But in B.o.Chillianwallah, punjab army was thoroughly defeated and in 1849 B's completely annexed Punjab in company's dominion.
