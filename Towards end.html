Towards end...

# Simon Commission:#

  • In general there was provision of review of indian constitutional scenario after every 10 years. But initially Lee commission recommended early review of GOI Act 1919.
  • Moreover in 1924, Alexander Muddiman committee recommended creation of India Statutory commission to review the constitutional scenario in India.
  • As per recommendation, ISC was constituted under the leadership of John Simon. It was an all white commission, not even a single indian the most moderate the most liberal was accommodated in the commission which was going to decide the constitutional fate of indians.
  • It was embarrassing for the entire indian class.
  • At this time, liberal federation of Tej Bahadur Sapru proposed Simon Boycott which was approved INC.
  • Hindu Mahasabha and ML also supported Simon boycott (Though there was a small division in ML under the leadership of Md. Shafi who was against Simon boycott but MAJ carried majority in the support of boycott)
  • It was INC which made Simon boycott as a popular movement.
  • The form of boycott included:
    • Protests
    • Processions
    • Black flag demonstration 
    • with an exhortation ÔÇ£Simon go back!ÔÇØ

Government Response to boycott:

  • It was lathi charge on the protesting crowd.
  • One of the bad incident related to it happened in Lucknow where GB Pant and JLN were thoroughly beaten. {Pant got paralyzed protecting JLN}
  • But the worst incident happened at Lahore where LLR was hit on chest and on 17th Nov he succumbed to the injuries.
  • After his death youth revolutionaries decided to take the revenge of his killing.

Gathering Storm (1927-1930):

  • In 1927, there was meeting of all India congress committee in Delhi.
  • In this meeting JLN proposed a snap resolution that congress must aim for Poorna Swaraj rather than Self rule.
  • Majority of the people agreed to this resolution. OTOH, huge protest against Simon commission started. Looking at the protest of indians then SoS Birkenhead challenged indians to come up their own constitutional scheme but make sure that all the major parties and communities are giving their consent to it.
  • Challenge was accepted and a committee was constituted under the leadership of MLN.

Nehru Report (Aug 1928):

  1. Dominion status objective for India.
  2. Responsible government in center and states.
  3. It objected separate electorate for muslim masses. and recommended that muslim masses can be given reservation in those areas where they are minority but not in numerical majority.
  4. Equal right to women including RtV.
  5. Administration of India must be completely secular in orientation.

Objection to Nehru Report:

  • There was vehement opposition of Dominion status provision from the side of JLN, SCB and Satyamurthy. They demanded Poorna Swaraj and went on to create India Independence League.
  • Hindu Mahasabha objected on the provision of secular state. 
  • ML rejected the provision of Separate electorate so as Sikhs and Anglo-Indians.

At this time congress maintained that they are giving 1 year space to british authority to implement dominion status but if they are not implementing it then we will aim for Poorna Swaraj.

Meanwhile, news related to conduct of RTC started to float. It was said that all the major parties of India and representatives of all the communities will be invited to Britain to have a equal level discussion with british authority.

Delhi Manifesto:

  • All the major leaders of congress met at Delhi to discuss news related to RTC.
  • They asked british authority that it must be made clear that the purpose of RTC is not to discuss when dominion status would be granted but to formulate the scheme for the immediate implementation of DS.
  • To this Qn, B's did not gave any reply as such the general consensus in congress was not to participate in RTC.

Annual Session of INC 1929 - (Lahore):

  • It was presided by JLN. In this session Poorna Swaraj declaration was made as the objective of congress.
  • It was also decided that congress will not participate in 1st RTC. 
  • Moreover, Gandhi was given full authority to initiate CDM.
  • As a mark of symbol of Poorna Swaraj declaration, tricolor flag was unfurled on the banks of river Ravi on 26th Jan 1930. Moreover on this day independence pledge was read out and it was collectively affirmed.

2nd Phase of Revolutionary Nationalism (1924-34):#

  1. Hindustan Republican Army:
    • It was founded by Sachindranath Sanyal and Yogesh Chatterjee.
    • The objective of this organization was not only to kill unpopular british officers but also to loot british treasury and raise funds for purchasing weapons.
    • Their very 1st activity was looting a train stationed at Kakori in Lucknow.
    • B's filed Kakori Conspiracy Case against revolutionaries. 
    • 4 responsible revolutionaries were arrested and immediately executed i.e. Rajendra Lhiri, Roshan Lal, Ramprasad Bismil, Asfaq-ullah-Khan.
  2. Hindustan Socialist Republican Association:
    • In 1928, Chandrashekhar Azad announced the formation of HSRA in Firoz shah Kotla, Delhi.
    • The objective of this organization became establishment of socialism in India. They also decided to abandon the policy of killing of unpopular british officers, informers and traitors.
    • The important members of this organization were Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, Sukhdev, Jatin das, Batukeshwar Dutta.
    • Bhagat Singh:
      • He was born in the family of nationalist on 28th Sept 1907 in Lahore.
      • His uncle Ajit Singh was one of the famous nationalist of the time and he was a close friend of LLR.
      • From his very childhood he had nationalist orientation. He was a voracious reader and highly influenced with the thought process of Karl Marx.
      • During his school days (DAV) he created Lahore Cchatra Sangha to promote nationalism.
      • He did not believed in singular heroics and further said that there cannot be any replacement of mass movement.
      • He hated communal politics and communalism. He did not believed in religion and in this regard he wrote a text ÔÇ£Why i am an AtheistÔÇØ
      • He went on to say ÔÇ£Political freedom is not the real freedom and that real freedom includes end of all exploitation i.e. external and internalÔÇØ
    • After the death of LLR, Bhagat Singh, shivaram rajguru and Sukhdev decided to take revenge of his killing and they killed one of the responsible officers John Saunders. After this incident british government issued shoot at sight order.
    • In another instance, Bhagat Singh and Batukeshwar Dutta threw a bomb in central assembly when public safety bill was getting passed. It was a low intensity bomb not intended to kill anyone but to make the deaf hear the sound.
    • They did not escaped from the place and quoted their arrest. After their arrest their trial started one of the revolutionary Jatin Das demanded treatment like political prisoners and went on in to an epic fast. It is said that on the 63rd day of his fast he succumbed.
    • OTOH, capital punishment was prescribed to bhagat Singh, R and S. On 23rd march 1931 they were executed.
  3. In Bengal:
    1. Gopinath Saha:
      • He tried to kill the hated police commissioner Charles Tegart in january 1924.
      • But due to some miscalculation he killed somebody else as a result he was over powered and executed.
    2. On 8th December 1930, Benoy Krishna Basu, Badal Gupta and Dinesh Gupta entered into police HQ at writers building at Calcutta. 
      • They were dressed like European officer and they intended to kill the head of prison guard NS Simpson. 
      • Though they were successful in their attempt but they have to loose their lives. Benoy and Dinesh shot themselves with their own revolvers and Badal Gupta took potassium cyanide.
    3. Two young girls Shanti Ghosh and Suniti Chaudhary successfully killed the hated district magistrate of Comilla, Charles Stevens. {interested in womens so took advantage of him}
    4. Another women revolutionary Beena Das Lt. Gov of bengal Stanley Jackson during her convocation ceremony. She made 5 fire shots but was unsuccessful in killing LG but she was able to seriously injure him.
    5. Indian Republican Army / Chittagong Group:
      • It was founded by Master Surya Sen. He intended to loot weapons from british armories.
      • He organized a raid in Chittagong armory. He was supported by two young girls Preety Lata Wadedar and Kalpana Dutta.
      • During raid Preety was shot dead. Master Surya Sen and Kalpana were able to escape.
      • After this incident SS started to operate from different different locations in disguised form. Bit unfortunately someone informed his location to B's which led to his arrest and finally he was executed on 12th Jan 1934. 

      • With his death the 2nd phase of revolutionary nationalism came to stand still.

# Civil Disobedience Movement:#

  • There was a challenge before Gandhi to choose a particular law which is to be offered to for Civil Disobedience. He picked salt act of 1882.
  • As per this act, indians were not allowed to manufacture salt and also they cannot collect salt form natural salt pans.
  • Indian were forced to purchase salt form british manufacturers and mrp of salt included a very heavy salt tax.
  • Gandhi felt that salt is one commodity with which all the indian households will feel connected and moreover braking the salt law will not involve any violence.

Salt March to Dandi:

  • On 12th March 1930, Gandhi started with 78 delegates from his Sabarmati ashram form Ahmedabad.
  • On 6th April 1930 he reached coast of Dandi and took handful of salt and declared that it is a crime against god and humanity to submit any longer before british authority.
  • It marked the beginning of CDM.

Developments during CDM:

  1. In TN, C Rajagopalachari organized salt march form triconapally to vedaranyam coast to break the law.
  2. In Ke, K. Kelappan organized it form Calicut to Payannur.
  3. In assam a band of satyagrahis started march form Sylhet to Naokhali Coast in Bengal.
  4. Sarojini Naidu along with Imam Sahib and Gandhi's son manilal started the march towards dharsana saltworks to break the salt law, but they were thoroughly beaten.
  5. In NWFP, Frontier Gandhi, Khan Abdul Gafar Khan organized CDM through his effective cadre of ÔÇ£Khudai KhidmadgarsÔÇØ aka Red Shirts. 
  6. During Peshawar demonstration, one incensed british officer ordered Gurkha regiment to fire on unarmed crowd but they refused to repeat the mistake of Jallianwala Bagh.
  7. In Assam, govt released Cunningham Circular which advised students not to participate in any national movt o/w they will have to face dire consequences. But students started to participate in national movt in much greater numbers.
  8. In Nagaland, Rani Gadinlieu, revolted against B's. It resulted in her arrest and she was kept in prison till India's independence.

Meanwhile 1st RTC was conducted in Britain w/o participation of congress. As such all the discussions of 1st RTC seemed to be abortive. There was a pressure on viceroy Irwin to bring congress to the table anyhow. At this time, Tej Bahadur Sapru and MR Jayakar facilitated talks b/n Gandhi and Irwin.

Gandhi Irwn Pact:

  1. Lenient treatment to govt servants who have resigned. 
  2. Remission of all the illegal fines not yet collected.
  3. return of confiscated land not yet sold to 3rd party.
  4. Restoring freedom of press.
  5. Right to make salt for consumption.
  6. Right to peaceful protest and picketing.
  7. (Not accepted) Congress demanded inquiry into police torture on people during CDM
  8. (Not accepted) Gandhi demanded that capital punishment given to bhagat Singh, rajguru, and sukhdev must be converted into lesser punishment.

Annual Session of INC (March 1931):

  • SVP became president of congress. In this session meaning of Poorna Swaraj was defined for masses. It said ÔÇ£Political freedom must include economic freedomÔÇØ ÔåÆ influenced by BS and socialism.
  • In the session two resolutions were introduced i.e. on 
    • Fundamental Rights
    • National Economic Plan
  • Gandhi was permitted to participate in 2nd RTC on behalf of congress.

2nd RTC (Nov 1931):

  • Gandhi along with Mahadev Desai participated in RTC. Even after powerful speech and advocacy Gandhi was not able to achieve anything.
  • He returned back to India empty handed.
  • During 2nd RTC, Winston Churchill went on to say ÔÇ£Gandhi was half-naked seditious FakirÔÇØ. When he returned back to India he faced severe criticism.
  • At this time, gujarati poet Zevarchand Meghani wrote for Gandhi ÔÇ£Even this last cup of poison you must drink BapuÔÇØ

Meanwhile in India, Irwin was replaced by new viceroy Willingdon, who was an imperialist to the core. He came to India with 3 policy considerations:

  1. Gandhi must not be allowed to resume CDM
  2. In general no national movement must be permitted to gather force.
  3. To reassure british loyalist that british government has not lost its will to rule in India and it is very much in tact.

When Gandhi resumed CDM, Willingdon came up with civil martial law. Civil liberties no longer existed, people were tortured and arrested according to whims and fancies of britishers.

Within 2 months >80k people were arrested, which was also leading to many violent instances form the different parts of the country. At this time Gandhi started his Harijan Tour to create awareness about upliftment of untouchables.

In Nov 1933, he shifted his ashram form Sabarmati to Wardha for harijan upliftment program. In April 1934, formally Gandhi withdrew CDM.

Communal Award and Poona Pact:

  • On 16th Aug 1932, British PM Ramsay McDonald announced communal award for depressed classes. According to this award, depressed classes were entitled separate electorate and status of minority.
  • Gandhi opposed it vehemently, he said that it is attack on Indian unity and nationalism. British want to break Hinduism. 
  • He further added that if depressed classes are to be treated as minority then no body will try to reform untouchability and untouchables will remain untouchables in perpetuity.
  • Gandhi announced his fast unto death against communal award.
  • Poona Pact:
    • Representative of depressed classes, DrA agreed for Poona pact with Gandhi.
    • As per the pact, separate electorate provision was to be abandoned and more seats were to be reserved in central and provincial legislative assembly.

# GoI Act 1935:#

Making / considerations:

  • Recommendation of Simon Commission
  • Deliberations of 3 RTCs
  • White Papers
  • Report of Joint Select Committee
  • recommendations of SoS Samuel Hoare.

Provisions:

  1. It declared to create All India Federation comprising of british indian provinces and princely states. but creation of federation was conditional on the fulfillment of two criteria
    1. Half of the princely states must give their consent to join federation
    2. The aggregate population in joining princely states must be more than ┬¢ of total population of all the princely states {none of the criterial was fulfilled as such, federation never became operational}
  2. Diarchy was removed form the provinces and provincial autonomy was sought to be provided.
  3. At center Diarchy was introduced as per this arrangement GGEC will manage reserved subjects and GGLC will manage transferred subjects.
  4. It created 3 list of legislations i.e. Federal, Provincial, and concurrent.
  5. Bicameral legislation at center was reconstituted with upper house and lower house. Moreover princely states were allocated 104 seats in upper house. Members of princely states were nominated and from british ruled states were elected.
  6. This act led to the establishment of:
    • Federal court of India
    • Federal bank of India
    • Federal PSC
    • Provincial PSC and Joint PSC.
  7. It enfranchised approx. 10 to 14% population of India.

Annual Session of INC 1936 (Lucknow):

  • It was presided by JLN. In this session all the members of congress equivocally rejected GoI Act 1935.

Annual Session of INC 1937 (Faizpur):

  • It was presided by JLN. This was the 50th session of congress and it was meant to discuss the problems of peasants of India.
  • But surprisingly there was no discussion on abolition of zamindari

Provincial elections 1937:

  • Though congress rejected GoI Act 1935 route and branch but all the congress members agreed that congress must fight elections to deepen anti imperial feeling.
  • But congress members differed on the question of ÔåÆ formation of ministries in provinces.
  • Leaders like JLN, SCB, and congress socialist leaders rejected the idea of congress ministries they said creation of ministry will be the negation of rejection of 1935 act.
  • Leaders like Rajagopalachari, MA Ansari and satyamurthi advocated formation of ministries
  • Gandhi's Stand:
    • Initially, he was hesitant on this topic but later after good election performance he was inclined to give a trial to congress ministries.
    • He advised them to ÔÇ£hold the offices lightly not tightlyÔÇØ 
  • Congress contested on 760 seats out of 1161 seats in 11 british indian provinces. Congress got clear majority in 6 states i.e. up, cp, bombay, madras, bihar, odisha. Congress formed coalition in 3 states i.e. Sindh, assam, nwfp.
  • In Punjab, unionist party of Sikandar Hayat khan formed the government.
  • In Bengal coalition government of ML and Krishak Praja party formed the government.

Work of Congress ministries:

  • They led by example and tried to adopt austere measures.
  • They controlled communal violence in their respective states and also tried to foster unity b/n H's and M's.
  • Political prisoners not convicted for violence were released form Jail.
  • Freedom of press was restored.
  • Ban on many organization was lifted eg. Hindustan Seva Dal and Youth League. But ban on Communist Party of India remained intact as it was imposed by central government.
  • Congress ministries tried to implement gandhian measures in respective states like Gandhian education policy, promotion of vegetarianism, protection of cattle's and discouraging intoxicating drinks.
  • Some of the states like UP came up with tenancy reform legislation. 
  • Industrial dispute bill was also introduced which was based on cooperation with labour class but not confrontation.

Few blemishes of congress ministries:

  1. Premier of madras Rajagopalachari arrested two left wing congress socialist leaders Yusuf Mehrally and SS Batliwala for giving inflammatory speeches against british authority. He was criticized for behaving like british.
  2. Home minister of Bombay KM Munshi used the services of CID to spy on left wing and communist leaders.

In Sept 1939, WW2 broke out and British authority declared India to be party in WW2 w/o taking connivance of Indian leadership. At this time Gandhi asked a question ÔÇ£How come an enslaved nation like India could be of any help to british against the fascist forces of Hitler?ÔÇØ

After 28 month rule congress ministries tendered their resignation.

# Towards Final Act:#

Annual Session of INC 1938 (Haripura):

  • SCB was elected as president of congress in this session. {SVP did not like Bose much due to the personal issue ÔåÆ Court case for the property of vitthal bhai patel}
  • Bose proposed creation of Planning committee to formulate the scheme of industrialization of India. JLN was chosen to be chairman of planning committee of India.
  • In this session Bose further declared his reservation against Gandhi's charkha policy. He rejected gandhian model of village centric model and promotion of small cottage industries.

  • the differences b/n Gandhi and Bose were clearly wide and out in this session.

Annual Session of INC 1939 (Tripuri):

  • Bose once again announced his candidature for congress president candidature. This time he knew that his candidature will be opposed by Gandhi.
  • Initially Gandhi approached JLN, who suggested name of Maulana Azad Sahib. Maulana Azad refused to contest against SCB. As such Gandhi came up with the name of Dr. Pattabhi Sitaramayya.
  • In presidential elections, Bose defeated Pattabhi Sitaramayya. Gandhi at this time said ÔÇ£this defeat is more mine then him(Dr. Pattabhi Sitaramayya) after all it was me who insisted his name when maulana Azad sahib refused to contestÔÇØ
  • Gandhi further said ÔÇ£I'm glad on his victory after all Subash babu is not the enemy of the country. He has suffered for it. In his opinion he has the boldest program and policies. in this scenario minority can only wish him best of luckÔÇØ.
  • immediately GB Pant moved a resolution reposing faith in the old working committee according to the wishes of Gandhi. This resolution got passed but Gandhi did not approved it. He said ÔÇ£He would not impose his choice on Bose and he is free to select his working committeeÔÇØ.
  • Such was the disastrous division in the congress that Bose decided to resign form presidents position. It led to election of Rajendra prasad as new president.
  • Bose created Forward Block to pursue the objective of India's freedom.

Pakistan declaration of ML (March 1940):

  • ML declared formation of separate nation Pakistan by joining geographically continuous areas in NW and east where Muslims are in majority.

August Offer 1940 by Viceroy Linlithgow:

  • by August 1940, Germany made huge advances in ww2 scenario. By this time Belgium, holland , and France has fallen to Germany.
  • It was compulsion of Britain to seek the cooperation of indian leaders.
  • Proposals:
    • Expansion of viceroy executive council to include more indians.
    • Creation of war advisory council, which will also include indian members.
    • After the war is over a representative body is created to frame the constitution of india.
    • No constitutional advancement will happen against the wishes of minority.
  • Later viceroy executive council was expanded and more indians were included in it.

Annual Session of INC 1940 (Ramgarh) ÔåÆ president Maulana Azad Sahib.

  • In this session congress reiterated its position on war that an enslaved nation like India could be of no help of Britain.
  • It asked british authority to accede to indian demands peacefully in this duration o/w when the organization would be fit enough for the purpose it will aim for civil disobedience.

Individual Satyagraha:

  • Gandhi sympathize with efforts of Britain in ww2 against fascist forces of Hitler. At this time he did not wanted to initiate any mass movement because it will embarrass Britain in its just causse.
  • Gandhi wanted to give ample time to british authority to accept indian demands peacefully.
  • But at the same time there was a need to give strong political expression to the public of India.
  • Individual satyagraha was a step in this direction. It was CDM but on personal level. It was all about emphasizing on freedom of speech and expression and also deepening anti-imperial feeling.
  • Satyagrahi's were to be chosen carefully so that their speeches must not lead to violent scenario.
  • Vinoba Bhave was the 1st, JLN was 2nd and Brahma Dutta was the 3rd satyagrahi.

There was a meeting of big leaders of world in Feb 1941, that included British PM Winston Churchill, Chinese leader Chiang-Kai-Shek and US President Roosevelt. In this meeting Roosevelt raised the qns on constitutional reforms in India and he also sympathizes with India's aspirations of freedom. 

Moreover there was pressure from other allies on Britain. In this situation Churchill decided to sent Cripps Mission to India. He chose senior labor politician Stafford Cripps, who had very good relationship with indian leaders and he also supported India's aspiration of self rule.

Cripps Mission:

  1. A Dominion of India would be setup. Indians will have option to remain in british commonwealth or they can secede form commonwealth in later times.
  2. Constituent assembly would be created to frame the constitution of India. Members would be elected from british indian provinces and nominated by princely states.
  3. If any of the province is not agreeing to the proposed constitutional scheme they will enjoy the right to sign a separate agreement regarding their future and they can also have a separate constitution. {Blueprint of Partition}
  4. Nothing would be transacted against the wishes of minority.
  5. For the present time defense of India would be in the hands of british authority.

 ÔåÆ Congress objected on Dominion Status, nomination by princely states and blueprint of partition.

Quit India Movement QIM (1942-1947):

  • By 1942, Japan emerged as the most powerful nation in ww2 scenario. It acquired almost all the European colonies in east Asia and SE Asia. Japan occupied Singapore and came closer to NE India.
  • Japan broke the myth of british invincibility in sea warfare. Japan's victory till Andamans created little bit of anxiety in India.
  • At this time Gandhi was also very agitated due to failure of Cripps mission. He said ÔÇ£Leave India to god, if that is too much leave her to anarchy. I would risk itÔÇªÔÇØ
  • On 8th Aug 1942, At Gowalia Tank in Bombay Gandhi addressed the nation.
  • Gandhi appealed to masses to initiate a non-violent CDM, do not pay any heed to the orders of british authority and act as an independent nation.
  • He also gave the slogan ÔÇ£DO or DIE !!ÔÇØ
  • But in the early morning of 9th of Aug, all the major leaders were arrested in a single sweep and they were lodged into different different jails.
  • In this situation, a young leader of congress Aruna Asaf Ali unfurled the indian flag at Gowalia Tank to start QIM. But from its very 1st day movement was leaderless.
  • Since the movement was leaderless it turned violent. Moreover british authority also responded with extreme violence and suppression.
  • Underground activity:
    • Not allowed to function in open, many underground networks started to emerge in various provinces of India.
    • The main leaders of this were Achyut Patwardhan, Biju Patnaik, RP Goeka, JP Narayan, Ram Manohar Lohia, Ram Nandan Mishra, Aruna Asaf Ali, Usha Mehta and Sucheta Keipalini.
    • The most daring activity of underground network was start of congress radio. Aruna Asaf Ali and Sucheta Kripalini were the organizer of radio and Usha Mehta was the announcer.

    • Ram Manoharr Lohia regularly broadcasted from congress radio and congress radio operated clandestinely form different different locations in Bombay city.

    • One of the women industrialist of India, Sumati Morarji helped Achyut Patwardhan, to evade from arrest by giving new car to him on daily basis.
    • The biggest contribution of underground activity was keeping the spirit of QIM in the very severe phase of suppression.
