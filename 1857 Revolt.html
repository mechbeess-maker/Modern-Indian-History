1857 Revolt

 # Causes#

It was the most massive of all the revolts. It was a landmark event in the Indian history which led to end of an old era and beginning of new era. It was the final culmination of 100 year long tradition of fearce resistance against british authorities. The causes of the revolt were intertwined together and it is difficult to determine the sequence of the revolt.
 
The circumstances prevailing in the 1857 did not occurred in the past and not tended to reoccur in the future. The underlying causes are as follows.
 
  1. Immediate Cause of revolt
     
    • British authority replaced old Brown Bess Musket with new enfield rifles for their greater range and accuracy. In the new rifle catridge was quoted with a paper and paper was smeared with cow and pig fat.
    • Before loading the catridge in rifle, paper was to be biten off. It hurted the religious sentiments of Hindua and Muslim sepoys alike.
    • When they refused to greased catridges, they were fired form the services.
    • Later they were also handed over hard punishments, which fuelled the revolt.
       
  2. Military causes of the revolt.
    • Enlistment Order of 1856
      • As per this order sepoys were supposed to serve where ever they are ordered to go i.e. even in the other british colonies.  
      • It led to doubt in the mind of sepoys, that in foreign land they will  be forced to embrace Christianity. Some of them were driven by superstitious belief that crossing the sea is a sin and they might loose membership of their community.
    • Earlier sepoys enjoyed free postage privilege but after the Post office act of 1850s this privilege was withdrawn.
    • Whenever a Sepoy served far from the home location he was given bhatta or allowances but after 1850 it was withdrawn.
    • An Indian sepoy in the army was paid 1/3rd the salary as compare to European sepoy. It was not a problem till additional privileges were given but after the withdrawal of privileges they started to feel relative deprivation.
    • There were increased instances of racial discrimination in the army and many a time sepoys were mistreated with the boots.
    • Majority of the recruits in the army were from bengal, awadh and north west frontier province. where caste and class consolidation was very high. Whenever upper caste Hindus and upper Muslims were humiliated before the lower class recruits, the upper caste and class felt that it was deliberate attempt of B's to break the traditional social fabric of India.
       
  3. Economic Causes of the revolt
    • Exploitative Land revenue systems
      • Under all the land settlements be it Permanent, ryotwari or mahalwari. there was heavy burden on peasants to pay the revenue on time. All of them were highly exploitative. All the revenue settlements led to peasants getting caught in vicious cycle of debt, increase in farmer suicide rate, commercialization of agriculture, commodification of land etc.
         
    • Despoilation of Indian Trade, Handicraft and artisan products
      • Right from the beginning of 18th century, b's enjoyed one way free trade but on the other hand Indian traders had to pay high amount of taxes when they are selling their products outside India.
      • The manufactured goods of Britain despoiled the Indian handicraft and artisan products.
         
    • Confiscation of rent free states.
      • In 1850, govt constitutes "Inam commission" to look into the title deeds of rent free estates.
      • Till 1856, 21000 cases were brought before commission. It ordered confiscation of all 21k rent free estates.
      • the people who were mostly affected with this confiscation were Maulvi's and Ullema's who gave religious services. When the revolt started they participated in large numbers.
         
  4. Socio-religious causes of the revolt
    • Right form the beginning, british policy was identified with certain degree of reform in Indian society. but the way these reforms were imposed, it created ambivalence towards old orthodox order in the society. People did not liked top-down approach of social reform {legal way to do this} and they also questioned that  b's have no rights to interfere in the social affairs of Indian society.
    • B's often disregarded rituals and customs of Hindu and Muslim society. They often called them as Barbaric and Bigots.
    • The Missionary activities and their forced religious conversions further created furore in society. 
    • in 1850, government came up with hindu religious disability act which tried to modify many of the rituals and customs of Hindus. It created doubts in minds of hindu society that they will be forced to embrace christianity.
       
  5. Political causes of revolt
    • The subsidiary alliance system of wellesely eroded the trust b/n B's and indian princely states. Indian rulers realised that they gave false promise of protection and in the guise of it they chipped off indian territory for payment. 
    • In 1848, GG Dalhousie came up with Doctrine of Lapse. As per policy if an indian ruler dies without a successor form direct blood then adoptions from the extended families will not be accepted and the native territories annexed into british indian territory. With the application of it following princely states were annexed: Satara(1848) -> Jaitapura -> sambhal -> Bhagat -> Chota Nagpur -> Jhansi -> Nagpur (1854). The annexation of these territories was done with utmost disrespect towards the native rulers. This wholesale swiping away of Indian territories were not liked by the subjects of these areas and once the revolt started they participated in huge numbers.
       

# Course of the revolt:#

  • The very first instance of refusal of use of greased catridges happened in Berhampur area.
  • In Feb 1857, 19th native Bengal infantry, refused to use the due rifles after which they were disbanded from the army.
  • In March 1857, Native infantry of Barrackpore, Calcutta refused to use the new rifle. One of the sepoy Mangal Pandey went a step ahead and killed his commanding officer. He was overpowered and executed.
  • The most important event happened on 23rd April 1857 in Meerut, where native cavalry refused to use the greased catridges. They were tried by a court martial and judgment was pronounced on 9th May. Uniforms of sepoys were stripped off and they were sentenced 10 years hard imprisonment. Moreover they were fettered and marched to jail. 
  • Next morning i.e. 10th may, other sepoy revolted and released the sepoys in Jail. By the evening they killed all the British officers in Meerut and marched towards Delhi.
  • 11th May they reached delhi and proclaimed mutiny in the name of Mughal emperor Bahadur Shah Jafar. 
  • Very soon revolt spread to entire north, western and eastern India. South India remained lesser affected with the revolt.
     

# Prominent Personalities:#

  • Delhi: the symbolic leadership was with mughal emperor Bahadur Shah but the original command lay in the hands of general Bakht Khan who brought his troops from Barelley.
  • Bareilly: the former ruler of Rohilkhand Khan Bahadur took charge in bareilly.
  • Kanpur: Original command was in the hand of Nana Sahib (aka Raja Bithoor), he was son of last peshwa Bajirao II. Tantya Tope was his most trusted aid. Nana Sahib was enraged with discontinuation of his family pension.
    • When the mutiny started he joined it. But in the later stages he fled to Nepal
  • Awadh/Oudh: Nawab Wajid Ali Shah of Lucknow was deported to Calcutta by british authority. After his deportation, Begum Hazrat mahal organised administration of Lucknow as the regent of her minor son Bijris Qadir. 
    • She organied administration with the help of both hindu and muslim official but B's started to interfare in the affairs of Lucknow. 
    • When the mutiny started BHM also joined the rank of mutineer's.
  • Faizabad-Ayodhya: 
    • the revolt was led by Maulvi Ahmadulla who was originally from Madras, but long back shifted to Faizabad.
    • He was offended by confiscation of his rent-free estate. He was one of the outstanding leaders of revolt.
  • Bihar:
    • Revolt from Bihar was organized by zamindaar of Jagdishpur Kunwar Singh. He was a man in 70s but regarded as one of the most outstanding leaders of the revolt. His zamindari estate was confiscated by B's as per sunsset clause.
  • Jhanshi:
    • Original name of Laxmi Bai was Manikarnika. She was born in a Marathi family at Benaras. Her father was a respected officer in peshwa Bajirao II court. A part of her upbringing happened in peshawas court. She was trained in many martial arts like a warrior. She got married to Raja Gangadhar Rao Navalkar of Jhansi who gave her the title of Laxmi Bai.
    • After the death of raja their adopted son was not recognized by B's and they annexed Jhanshi by application of DoL 
    • Driven out of Jhanshi, rani gave a battle cry of "Mai apni Jhansi nahi Dungi". She got the support of Tantya Tope and fought bravely with the britihsers. She martyred during the combat. After the suppression of revolt, British General Hugh Rose paid homage to Rani by saying ÔÇ£...the Indian mutiny has produced but one MAN and that man is a WOMAN...ÔÇØ 
In the course of revolt Tantya Tope was captured and hanged by B's. Maulvi Ahmadulla was killed by a pro british raja of Shahjahanpore. Kunwar Singh died in the battlefield during the combat.
 

# Reasons for the failure of the revolt:#

  1. There was absence of feeling of pan India nationalism at this time.
  2. The ideology behind the revolt did not prove to be effective. The idea of violent overthrow of british rule could only happen when there is a central leadership and common ideology.
  3. There wasn't any effective communication but most of the time primitive mode of communication was used.
  4. The revolt happened in an Ad-hoc fashion. and it was not happening in a seamless way.
  5. Revolt was parochial in outlook whereby everyone was fighting for their own self interest.
  6. Revolt didn't spread to southern India in a way it was expected. 
  7. Many of the rulers and prominent zamindars became collaborator with the B's and supported suppression of revolt.
  8. The intelligentsia did not supported the revolt as they felt it was backward looking trying to re-establish the old feudal order 
  9. OTOH, B's operated under a central command, effective organization and a very clear communication. The commander in chief of British forces was regularly informed about rebel activities through telegraph services. B's were also laces with sophisticated weapons.
     

# Significance of the revolt:#

  • There was complete cooperation b/n H's and M's during the course of revolt.
  • It led to complete transfer of power form BEIC to British Crown.
  • Even in its failure its served great purpose by implanting the seed of nationalism which further facilitated the creation of many regional organizations and finally leading to the creation of an All India organization to fight for indians.
  • British authority became cautious after the suppression of revolt and started to indians in many administrative roles.
