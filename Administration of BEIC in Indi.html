Administration of BEIC in India

# Administration before 1773
  • The highest decision making body of the company was 24 Court of Directors. They were appointed by shareholders of company or court of properietor's on annual basis. 
  • Cod created many committees to look after each and every aspect of the company.
  • In India company created 3 presidencies i.e. Bombay, Bengal and Madras. Each of the presidencies were absolute government in themselves, which means they were completely autonomous. Independent of each other and did not needed consultation with each other. They were only liable to report to 24 CoD.
  • In each of presidency, a president/Governor was the highest position. GG in Council was the highest decision making body of presidency. Each and every decision of a presidency was taken with the Majority vote of GG in Council.
# Regulating Act of 1773
 
      Background:
  • After the t.o.Allahabad, dual system of government became operational in bengal. As per the system Diwani -> B's and Nizamat -> British controlled bengal Nawab. This led to double harassment of people of bengal. Apart from it, in 1770, a major famine happened in bengal which swiped near about 1/3rd population of bengal.
  • At this point of time, people expected relief from British authorities but it was not provided. Moreover there was very heavy corruption in the company. Company officials were amassing huge amount of corrupt wealth for themselves.
  • At this time British parliament appointed a secret committee to look into the cases of corruption. The committee reported that company officials had aggregated so much of corrupt wealth that they can be better called as "Nawab's of London".
  • At this time British parliament did not wanted to be mute spectator when the people of bengal in hapless situation and reeling under the pressure.
  • In 1772, company applied for 1 million pound loan in the British parliament as the company was on the verge of bankruptcy {due to corruption}. The British crown represented by parliament grabbed this opportunity to check the accounts of the company and regulate its affairs.
     
      Provisions:
  1. G of B -> GG of B. Governors of Madras and Bombay were made subservient to him, which means they needed to report and consult with GG of B.
  2. To assist GGofB, GG Executive council was made with total 5 members. All the decisions were taken with majority  vote. 
  3. It banned private trade by company officials.
  4. Every company employee on his return home needed to give a detailed statement regarding the wealth and property he has acquired and how he has acquired in India.
  5. Company officials were prohibited in taking any present or gift from the natives of India.
  6. It called for establishment of Supreme Court at Calcutta. Accordingly, SC was estd in 1774 with 1 Chief Justice and 3 other Judges. Elizah Impey was the 1st CJ of SC.
     
      Analysis:
  1. No where in the act any welfare measure for the people of Bengal was mentioned.
  2. GG was rendered powerless in his own council as very often the 4 Executive members ganged together to block the decisions of GG.
  3. Corruption in company and private trade continued as it is. Since there was no mechanism to stop pvt trade.
  4. Often there was clash b/n the judicial powers of GG and SC CJ. {overlapping jurisdiction}
  5. Bombay and Madras presidency were still acting on their own discretion and hardly they consulted to Bengal GG.
     
# Amending Act of 1781 -> Place the Judicial powers of GG above the Judicial powers of CJ of SC.
 
# Pitts India Act (1784): 
It was named after British PM William Pitt Younger. This act tried to correct the issues of Regulating Act.
  1. It created a new body in Britain k/as Board of Control (BoC) with 6 members. British cabinet minister Secretary of state was made president of BoC. 
  2. BoC was supposed to supervise the functioning of CoD. It led the beginning of Joint rule of British Crown and EIC in India.
  3. It differentiated the commercial and political functions of the company. {like commercial account and political account were separated so that functions of company can be monitored effectively}
  4. For the 1st time company's territory in India was referred as British Possession in India.
  5. One Executive member was removed from GGEC so as if GG gets support of 1 Ex. member then with his casting vote he can go ahead with his decision.
  6. One of the provision of this act is that it prohibited interference in the native politics of the Kingdoms.
     
      Analysis:
  • A nebulous connection of corruption developed b/n BoC and CoD.
  • No check on pvt trade was effected by this time.
  • Even after removing 1 executive member, GG was still powerless in his council.
-> NOTE: In 1785, Cornvallis was appointed as new GG, he demanded overriding power in his council. As per the special act of British parliament, cornvallis was given over riding power in his council.
 
# Charter Act of 1793:
 
  1. It renewed the company's charter for next 20 years.
  2. The over riding power given to Cornvallis was further extend to all the GG's.
  3. British felt that Indian administration must be based on the written laws instead of personal rule of the rulers.
  4. It further called for translation and publication of written laws in the vernacular languages of India so that people can know about their rights and immunities.
  5. It also maintained that salaries of GG and his GGEC will be paid from Indian revenues and not from British exchequer.
     
# Charter Act of 1813:
 
      Background:
  • By the end of 18th century, Rise of Napoleon happened in France and Europe. He was arch rival of Britain. He created a continental system with which a large number of European countries got associated. 
  • In 1806 and 1807 -> He released the Decree of Berlin and Milan respectively. Whereby he banned any kind of trade with Britain.
  • By this time Britain was at the peak of Industrial Revolution and it was manufacturing hub of the world. With the declaration of Napoleon the entire European market became unavailable for british manufacturers. At this time these manufacturers started to create pressure on British parliament to end the Monopoly of the BEIC in India and China.
  • They demanded access to these markets. They also quoted Adam smith's theory of "Laissez Faire" or вАЬFree Trade TheoryвАЭ
      Provision:
  1. It renewed the company's charter but company's monopoly of trade in India ended.
  2. However, company retained its monopoly in tea trade and trade with China.
  3. It clearly declared that the intention of British authority in India is to establish British paramountcy in India whereby British power would be paramount and supreme and Indian powers would be subordinate to B's.
  4. It allowed christian missionaries to settle in India under a license and they were entitled to promote moral and religious education in India.
  5. It allocated 1 lakh rupees from Indian revenue for the purpose of education in India. {However this money was not made available completely till 1833. In 1820s little bit of money was released as per Instrument of Instruction}
# Charter Act of 1833
 
  1. It renewed the company's charter for next 20 years but it completely ended the company's monopoly of trade in India and china.
  2. The Company was asked to wind up it commercial operations as soon as possible. However, the company was allowed to retain its political territory and administer it, but it was held in the name of her Majesty.
  3. It formally legalized colonization of India by Europeans.
  4. It added one Law member or Legislative expert in GGEC. His work was entirely rule making and he was not entitled as the permanent member of the council {no right to vote} -> Macaulay was the 1st law member of the council.
  5. It called for creation of 1st law commission to codify Indian Penal code civil and criminal procedure code. Accordingly 1st law commission was created under the chairmanship of Macaulay.
    • Section 87 of the act provided for education and employment of Indians. It further maintained that there would be no discrimination on the basis of religion, PoB, caste, race in the matters of employment.
  6. It designated GGofB -> GGofIndia; William Bentick was the 1st GG of India. He was the most powerful GG till this date. All the political and financial power were given to GG and GGEC. He can repeal/alter/ammend any law or regulation. His actions can only be disapproved by Cod who were acting under the supervision of BOC.
  7. It created the local government of Agra by separating for the Bengal.
  8. It called for upliftment of slaves in India.
  9. It further said while drafting any law rules related to Indian marriage and the rights of heads of families must be taken into consideration.
      NOTE: It recommended that Hailebury College in Britain must create a scheme of Quota to include future Indian civil servants. but this suggestion was disregarded.
 
      Importance of Charter Act of 1833:
 
  • It was the watershed movement in the Indian history. It led to centralization of Indian Administration, which further led to political and administrative unification.
  • This act made it clear that company is loosing its relevance in India
  • for the 1st time it was mentioned taht Indians would be included in the administration without any discrimination.
  • For the first time separation of work was recognized. for eg: Law expert for legislation.
# Charter Act of 1853:
 
  1. It renewed the charter of the company but for the first time did not fix any time limit.
  2. It created a separate GG Legislative Council with 6 new members.
  3. It started the system of Local representation in GG Legislative's Council. Out of the 6 new members 4 were to be added from the local governments of Bombay, Madras, Bengal, and Agra. The condition was nominated members must have completed minimum 10 years in the service of BEIC.
  4. It reduced the number of CoD from 24 to 18. Out of which 6 were to be nominated by the British crown.
  5. It called for recruitment of Indians in Covenanted Civil Services. In this regard Macaulay committee was constituted to create the scheme and syllabus of exam.
-> Satendranath Tagore was the 1st Indian to clear this exam in 1864.

# Civil Services
The word "civil" was used to denote the revenue services of the company. It was used to differentiate military aspect from revenue aspect.
  • Cornvallis is Regarderd as creator of Modern Civil services in India. When he came to India, he observed company's civil servants have become highly corrupt. These civil servants were appointed by CoD's which often led to nepotism and favoritism. Moreover private trade was also important reason for corruption
  • On one hand effectively banned pvt trade and on the other hand he also raised the salaries of Indian Civil servants and they became the Highest paid civil servants in the world.
  • He also ensured promotions are happening on the basis of seniority.
  • Cornvallis was highly prejudiced against Indians and he made a statement that
     
    All the natives of Hindustan are corrupt
  • He led to Europeanization of Civil services. In 1793, he laid an official rule , that all the higher post in administration worth 500 pound annual salary will be held only by the British.
  • It was followed probably because of the following reasons:
    • There was high demand and popularity of Indian Civil Services among the B's in Britain.
    • Cornvallis thought that Indians dont fit in the British scheme and they will not serve the British interest will full integrity.
    • He was highly convinced that British colonial interest  will be better served by British only.
  • Cornvallis also relieved the district collector from his judicial functions and now he was enterest only for the revenue administration.
  • In 1798, when wellesely came to India he decided to create a civil servant training college at Fort William Calcutta. But later his actions were disapproved by court of Directors and they created a separate civil servant training college at Hailebury in England.
# British Army
 
There were two branches of company army in India: the Queens Army and Company's Troops
By the time of the 1857 revolt in the company's army 86% Indians were there. It is very surprising that how handful of Britishers were able to control such a large Indian nature army. Followng reasons can be cited for it.
  • At that time there was no spirit of pan-india nationalism.
  • It was a good opportunity for employment for Indians as british recruited from all the sections of society.
  • British were good pay masters as such Indians served as good mercenaries.
  • Indians had the tradition of loyalty towards salt, which they kept till their relation was organic with british.
  • All the higher positions were kept for british so that British can maintain effective control over army.
# Police Services
In the pre british India law and order was maintained in Ad-hoc manner. There were many officers who were responsible for law and order function.Eg: 
  • At village level -> Chowkidar, Fauzdar, and Amils
  • Zamindari Area -> zamindars were responsible for law and order function. Zamindars maintained a troop of Thanedars for the purpose.
Cornvallis noted that very often that these thanedars gave free hand to Dakaits who looted the villages towns and they shared the loot with zamindar. He revealed zamindar form law and order function and decided to create police organization for the purpose.
  • He divided districts into many circles k/as Thana. At thana one Indian person was appointed as in-charge k/as Daroga.
  • At the district level he created the office of Superintendent of Police
Later in 1833, William Bentick abolished office of SP and gave the charges of SP to district collector.

# Permanent Settlement
 
  • It is also c/as Zamindari Bandobast. It was initiated by Cornvallis in 1793 in the area of Bengal Bihar in the beginning but later it was extended to Odisha, Northern districts of Madras and Varanasi.
  • It covered near about 19% of area of British India.
  • The settlement was b/n british authority and zamindar that's why it was named Zamindari Bandobast. Since the revenue was permanently fixed it was also called permanent settlement.
  • From the permanently collected fixed amount 10/11th was the share of B's and 1/11 for the zamindar.
  • There was no fixation of rates which peasants will be paying to zamindars. 
  • Land ownership right was given to zamindar and it was hereditary and transferable. But the ultimate ownership of the land lay in the hand of british authority.
  • One crucial part of the settlement was the Sunset Clause. If zamindar is not able to make the payment on time it will lead to confiscation of his landed state and his zamindari is also snatched.
  • In case of crop failure due to any natural calamity, No relief was provided.
      Reasons for its implementation:
  1. Cornvallis wanted a permanently fixed amount for long period of time. 
  2. He anticipated that in the longer run when the tax rates are not increasing, the settlement would be beneficial to the peasants and they will have more saving to invest in land fertility.
  3. Cornvallis did not wanted to invest the british energy in the hard task of revenue collection but he wanted to focus on expansion of british regime.
  4. They wanted to create a paradigm shift in Indian society whereby B's will be creating a new class of zamindar who will be friendly but also loyal to british raj.
     
      Impact of settlement:
  • British:
    • It fulfilled the targeted objectives of B's to get a permanent amount for a longer period of time. but later B's were repentant that they must have kept the provision of revision of land revenue rates in future (It was inherent in the law that B's cannot Increase revenue rates even if they wish to).
       
  • Zamindars:
    • A good number of zamindars lost their land ownership right due to sunset clause. But majority of the zamindars reaped huge benefits. 
    • It also led to the beginning of Subfeudalization in Indian society and rise of large number of intermediaries and Absentee landlords.
       
  • Peasants:
    • It led to economic decline of peasantry of bengal. Their situation became worse than hand to mouth existence. There was pressure on them to pay the revenue on time for which they started to take loans form moneylenders on exorbitant interest rates.
    • As a result many of them were caught in vicious cycle of debt. It further led to increase in peasant suicide but also increased instances of revolt against british authority and zamindars.
       
  • Land:
    • One of the biggest casualty of the settlement was the land fertility of bengal. B's were not at all concerned about investment in land and they left it to zamindars. but zamindars were more concerned about increasing their profits. 
    • Peasants were not  the owners of land and they were not in a good economic situation to support the fertility of land. As such the agricultural land of bengal started to loose its fertility.
       
# Ryotwari Settlement
 
  • In 1792, two british officers, Thomas Munro and Captain Reed implemented this settlement in the area of Baramahal in Karnataka as an experimental project. But it took time for the settlement to be implemented on a broader level. 
  • In 1820, Thomas Munro implemented in Madras presidency and Elphinston implemented it in Bombay presidency.
  •  It covered approx 51% area of the British India. 
      Provision:
  1. The settlement was concluded b/n B's and peasants i.e. Ryots that's why it was named Ryotwari.
  2. Land ownership right was given to the peasants it was hereditary and transferable. But the ultimate ownership of land lay in the hands of British authority.
  3. If the peasants defaulted on the taxes it led to confiscation of their land and snatching of their land ownership right.
  4. The settlement necessitated the establishment of a big revenue department for the collection of revenue. As such a large number of people were recruited for the purpose.
  5. It provided relied to the peasants in case of crop failure due to any natural calamity. 
  6. They kept a provision of revision in land revenue rates after an interval of 30 years.
    calculation and imposition of taxes a comparative scientific method was adopted. Whereby the total expense on growing a particular crop was to be deducted from the total worth of the crop and on the remaining amount taxes were to be imposed. 
  7. It was further said that the maximum rate of taxes would be 50% and it must not exceed 50%.
     
      Reasons for implementation:
  • They wanted to promote cultivation of cash crops in India. So that raw material can be sent to Britain to feed their industries.
  • As per the utilitarian thought, land ownership was given to the peasants so that he develops emotional attachment to land and he may work extra to keep the fertility of land intact.
  • It was also thought that if something if left in the hands of peasants then only he will be interested in cultivating the cash crop.
  • It was a learning from permanent settlement that they must keep scope of revision in land revenue rates.
  • It was a good measure to keep direct control over the peasantry over India.
      Impact of this settlement:
 
  • British:
    • It fulfilled all the targeted objectives of british authority like supply of raw material to british industries, a good sustainable income and direct control on Indian peasantry. But it also increased the administrative cost of revenue collection.
    • It led to Commercialization of Agriculture which means cash crops were promoted instead of food grains. Which led to scarcity of food grains and further inflation in food grain prices.
       
  • Peasants:
    • The impact of settlement on peasants was no different than the other settlement. The tax burden was high and in case of crop failure they were not given any relief. 
    • Though the settlement did not include intermediaries on paper but in the process intermediaries got their positions.
    • When the big revenue department estd. village elites like Poligara and Mirasidars comfortably placed themselves in the revenue ranks. They became agents of british authority. After getting their appointments they started to use insider information for their own good. they started to purchase irrigated lands on cheaper rates and they also resorted to torture and extortion.
    • In 1855, Madras Torture Commission was created. It warned British authority if the grievances of Peasents is not addressed then it will implode into a bigger revolt.
       
# Mahalwari Settlement
  • It was introduced by Holt Mckenzi in 1819 in the western UP. Later by 1822, Martin Bird extended it to rest of India. It covered near about 30% area of british India.
      Provisions:
  1. It had mixture of both permanent and Ryotwari system. 
  2. For the collection of revenue, Mahal as fiscal unit was chosen. Mahal was group of villages.
  3. Revenue collection rights/agreements were signed with village headman, Thikadar's, Lambardar's.
  4. Land ownership right was given to the peasants. It was hereditary and transferable. But the ultimate ownership of land lay in the hand of British authority.
  5. From the collected revenue, british share was 2/3rd and rest was for the revenue collectors.
  6. Tax rate on peasants varied on the basis of land holding. Taxes were calculated after the land survey and measurement and land record was also maintained.
  7. There was provision of revision in revenue rates after an interval of 30 years.
      Reasons for its implementation:
  • As per utilitarian thought land ownership was given to the peasants. 
  • B's wanted to encourage cash crops like sugarcane and cotton in this area to feed the British industries.
  • To save administrative expense in collection of revenue, contracts were signed with intermediaries.
  •  Learining from ryotwari they also kept provision for revision in revenue rates after an interval of 30 years.
      Impact of settlement:
  • All the boxes for British authority were rightly ticked in this settlement and they reaped huge benefits so as saved the administrative cost of collection.
  • It also benefited the intermediaries
  • It led to commodification of land which means land became a commodity to be easily sold and Purchase in the land market. {B's authority facilitated the creation of land market in which rate of the land was decided by them}
  • Land survey was at the core of the settlement that it failed miserably. Faulty land records were created on the basis of idiosyncretic estimate but not on the basis of actual measurement.
  • The agricultural depression of 1829 further aggravated the problems of peasants of north India. Their anger and frustration was to be expressed rather loudly in the revolt of 1857.
