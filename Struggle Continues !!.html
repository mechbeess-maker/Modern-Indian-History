# Early phase of Gandhi in South Africa:#

  • G studied law from Britain when he returned back to India, he was unemployed for many months.
  • Finally in 1893, he got opportunity to fight a case in SA.
  • He went SA to sort out legal problems of his client Dada Abdulla (Gujarati Merchant).
  • During his stay he observed 3 types of indians
    • Indentured laborer's from south India who are working in south African mines.
    • Gujarati Muslim Memon merchants
    • Their children's who were born in SA but did not enjoyed any privileges.
  • They were not able to oppose british authority due to their non efficiency in English language. Moreover they accepted slavery as a way of life.
  • Gandhi offered them to teach English and make them efficient in English language.
  • He himself faced racial discrimination in SA. During his one of travels Pretovia вЖТ Johnsburg, he purchased 1st class  ticket but on the complain of white man he was bundled out form train. He was forced to travel in drivers box. Once he reached Johannesburg he started searching for room and he did not got any as such he has to spend his night on railway station.
  • When Gandhi solved the problems of dada Abdulla, he decided to go back to India. At this time Indians requested to Gandhi to stay a little more and solve the problem of indians which is basically the issue of dis-enfranchise Indians
  • Gandhi decide to stay a little more but his stay went on to linger for 20 years.

Passive Resistance phase of Gandhi (1894-1906):

  • In 1894, he founded Natal Indian Congress, to fight against the oppressive measures adopted by british authority against indians.
  • Initially he adopted moderate methods like prayers, petitions, sending request to South African legislature and colonial secretary in London.
  • in 1899, he created ambulance corps of indians during 2nd Boer War. He provided medical support to b's who were injured or afflicted in this war. 
  • Impressed by services of Gandhi british government conferred upon him title of Kaiser-i-hind.
  • In 1903, Gandhi released his 1st magazine on Indians вАЬIndian OpinionвАЭ in which he regularly highlighted the atrocities on indians.
  • Once again in 1906, he created Ambulance cops of indian during Zulu war but this time no favor was returned by british authority.

Satyagraha Phase (1906-1914):

A Satyagrahi is a person who will never bow down before any evil or unjust law. He will remain fearless, courageous but he will not resort to violence.

The main issues of this time were:

  • It was compulsory for indians to carry the registration certificates all the time.
  • A restriction was imposed on their movement they were not allowed to move out from Natal Province.
  • There was indirect promotion of Christianity and those marriages which were not conducted according to Christian methods were invalidated. At this time Gandhi suggested indians to cross Natal province and enter into Transvaal

Inspired by John Ruskin's book вАЬUnto this LastвАЭ, Gandhi created Phoenix farm which is considered as birthplace of satyagraha. It was also the place where satyagrahi's were trained.

He was also highly inspired form Leo Tolstoy's book вАЬKingdom of God is within youвАЭ. To provide alternative way of living he founded вАЬTolstoy FarmвАЭ with his German friend Kallenbach.

Finally in 1910-1914, round of negotiations happened b/n Gandhi, General Smut, Viceroy Hardinge and CF Andrews. Which resulted into acceptance of majority of the Gandhi's demand.

A satisfied Gandhi started his journey back to India and landed on 9th January 1915. This day is celebrated as Pravasi Bhartiya Diwas in current times. {It was started on 9th January 2003}.

# Home Rule Movement#

In 1914, tilak was released form Jail and he wanted to restart his political activity in India. This time he was very cautious and appealed to indians to cooperate with british authority during WW1. Ms. Annie Besant also wanted to initiate a political movement against british rule in India.

Home Rule movement was indian response to WW1 but in much organized manner than the Ghadar movement. During WW1 there was requisitioning of food supplies particularly wheat, rice, pulses. It led to scarcity of food grains, demand supply gap, high inflation in food prices, and life living cost increased.

In this scenario, Tilak and Annie Besant wanted to bring back accountability of British authority through Home rule movement.

Tilak's League:

  • Tilaks league became operational in April 1916, in the areas of Central Province, MH, KN, Bidar, Berar. It had total 6 branches and it was highly organized.
  • Tilak's league had 3 demand:
    1. Swaraj (Self-rule)
    2. Creation of linguistic states
    3. Education in vernacular medium

Annie Besant's League:

  • It became operational in Sept 1916 and it was operational in rest of India including Bombay. It had more than 200 branches but as compared to Tilak's league it was loosely organized.
  • With league of Annie Besant large number of indian nationalist got associated like Motilal Nehru, Jawaharlal Nehru, MMM, MAJ, Bipin Chandra Pal, Tej Bahadur Sapru, Bholabhai Desai, Gaurishankar Mishra, Kedarnath Pandey etc.
  • George Arundale was the secretary of this league and CP Wadia was important officer.

Objectives & Methods:

  • They wanted to create political awareness in society and spread a political education so that people can become assertive about their rights.
  • They adopted unique methods of creating awareness like Public Speeches, organizing libraries, reading rooms, propaganda through posters and pamphlets, and also dramatic depiction through street plays.

Response towards HRM:

  • A case was filed against Tilak but this time he was successfully defended by MAJ.
  • British came very hard on Annie Besant. They arrested so as Wadia and Arundale.
  • After the arrest of Annie Besant there was huge protest and movement all over India demanding release of Annie Besant.
  • At this time SoS Montague observed and commented вАЬShiva cut his wife into 52 pieces only to discover that he had 52 wivesвАЭ This is what happens to GoI when it interns Ms. Annie Besant.
  • There was no option other than releasing Annie Besant form Jail.
  • At the height of her popularity, on the suggestion of Tilak she became 1st women president of congress in 1917.

Fading out of Home Rule Movement:

  • in 1918, SoS Montague announced future reforms that will come in 1919, Annie Besant accepted it and called off her movement.
  • A british journalist, Valentine Chirole claimed that Tilak was вАЬFather of Indian UnrestвАЭ. Tilak went to Britain to file a defamation case against him. He was away for several months form India and when he returned back he was terminally ill as such his HRM became non functional.

Significance of HRM:

  1. It created a large group of ardent nationalist who played a vital role in the upcoming decades.
  2. It created organizational link between the nationalist leaders in the urban space and masses in the ruler areas.
  3. The organizational framework created by HRM leaguers was to be utilized later by Gandhi in his movements
  4. It increased the reach of print media and journalism even to the countryside of India.

Annual Session of INC Lucknow (1916):

  • It was presided by Ambika Charan mazumdar. This session led to unification of Moderates and Extremists congress. 
  • Moreover for the 1st time Muslim League participated in the annual session of congress, which led to historic Lucknow pact b/n Congress and ML.
  • Congress accepted separate electorate for Muslim masses and INC also agreed that Muslims must be given 1/3rd representation in central and provincial legislative council.
  • Analysis of Lucknow Pact:
    • (+) It allayed the fear of minority to be dominated by majority and for shorter period of time. It bridged the gap b/n Hindu and Muslim masses.
    • (-) INC was unable to understand that approval to separate electorate may create domino effect in the society where by ppl of other religion, caste, and community will also demand separate electorate and then congress will have no ground to reject it. It may lead to Balkanization of India.

# Early phase of Gandhi in India:#

Regional interventions:

Gandhi returned back to India on 9th January 1915 form South Africa. In his initial years he did not participated in any national movement.

On the question of HRM he said he would join any organization to affect its policy and not to be affected by it. (Thus he did not participated in HR agitation). Gandhi decided to tour all over India to understand grievances of people of India.

American thinker вАЬHenry David ThoreauвАЭ was the progenitor of idea of civil disobedience. But Gandhi was 1st to implement this.

After his initial ventures he decided to address some of the regional problems which are as follows:

  1. Champaran Satyagraha 1917:
    • European planters involved cultivators of Champaran into an agreement k/as Tinkathia System. As per this agreement cultivators were forced to grow indigo on 3/20th of their land holding.
    • In 1890s German synthetic blue dye were invented and it completely forced out indigo form the market.
    • Now it was necessary for European planters to release cultivators from obligation of growing indigo but they turned necessity into opportunity. They started demanding illegal release price form the cultivators.
    • At this point of time a local leader Rajkumar Shukla persuaded Gandhi to intervene. As such Gandhi decided to visit Champaran. Once Gandhi was there, he was forbidden by district magistrate,
    • He disobeyed him and entered into Champaran to start his investigation.{1st example of civil disobedience}

    • Gandhi got the support of many of the regional leaders of Bihar which includes names like Rajendra Prasad, Narhari Parikh, JB Kriplani, Mazhar-ul-Haq, and Mahadev Desai {greatest follower of Gandhi and his secretary}.

    • Gandhi's investigation created pressure on british authority and they passed an order where by European planters were instructed to stop taking illegal money and to return at least 25% of illegal money that they have taken from cultivators.
  2. Ahmedabad Mill Strike 1918:
    • Problem at Ahmedabad was discontinuation of Plague bonus and non-increment in the wages of workers in the very long time.
    • At this time workers persuaded Gandhi to intervene.
    • Gandhi met one of the mill owners, Ambalal Sarabhai and he tried to convince him to submit entire issue to a tribunal.
    • initially Ambalal agreed for it but later other mill owners refused. At this time Gandhi decided to go on hunger strike to create pressure on mill owners.{1st example of hunger strike}
    • It created pressure on mill owners and they agreed to submit the entire issue to the tribunal.
    • Tribunal gave its judgement whereby it said 35% increase in the wages is justified.
    • {in this issue one the biggest supporter of Gandhi was Anusuya Behan who was the sister of Ambalal Sarabhai}
  3. Kheda Satyagraha 1919:
    • Problem at Kheda was crop failure due to which peasants demanded remission of land revenues.
    • Earlier, GKGs Servants Society of India conducted a survey in Kheda and justified demand of peasants. 
    • At this time Gandhi was member of Gujarat Sabha and he played a vital role in this agitation. He was supported by the popular regional leaders of Kheda i.e. Sardar Patel, Vitthalbhai Patel, and Indulal Yagnik.
    • Gandhi advice to peasants to withhold the revenue and fight with evil system till their last breath.
    • Gandhi's appeal created an electrifying effect and ppl stopped paying taxes. It also brought embarrassment to british authority. 1st they suspended collection of revenue and also passed an instruction those who can afford to pay can pay the revenue.

GoI Act 1919 / Montague-Chelmsford Reforms:

  1. It introduced Dyarchy in the provinces, which means two authority i.e. popular ministers and executive councilors.
  2. It created two type of subjects i.e.
    1. Reserved Subjects вЖТ were kept under the administration of GG and his ex council. It included subjects like finance, law order, land revenue and irrigation.
    2. Transferred Subjects вЖТ were under popular ministers and it included subjects like education, Agriculture, health, industry, and local govt.
  3. It created 3 list of legislation i.e. Federal, Provincial and Concurrent.
  4. It introduced Bicameral legislation at center.
  5. It maintained that women can be given voting rights conditional to the discretion of provincial governmets.
  6. In case of failure of constitutional machinery in the provinces, administration of transferred subjects was to be done by executive councilors under the leadership of governor. 
  7. It extended separate electorate for Sikhs, Anglo-Indian and India Christians.

# Rowlett Bill and Rowlett Satyagraha#

This bill was named after Sydney Rowlett who was the chairman of committee of Bill. It was aimed at curtailing Civil liberties of indians in the name of curbing terrorist violence. The timing of bill was very wrong so as the intention.

It was the time when indians were expecting more political concessions and privileges british authority disappointed indian leadership.

At this time Gandhi suggested to initiate all India satyagraha. This movement will include protest, mass hartal, procession, fasting and prayers.

The official date of movement was 6th April 1919 but in north indian particularly in Delhi, Ambala, Jalandhar, Amritsar and Lahore it started little early which also led to violence on streets.

As a result B's arrested two popular leaders from Amritsar Dr. Satyapal and Saifuddin Kitchlew.

Jallianwala Bagh Massacre:

On 13th April 1919, Baisakhi day large number of ppl gathered in Amritsar to celebrate the festivity. A good number of people started attending a public speech at an open ground of Jallianwala. It is the moment when commanding officer of Amritsar General Dyre ordered his regiment to fire upon unarmed croud until their bullets are exhausted.

The official estimate of govt was 379 dead but real count was more than 1000 dead.

Entire country was stunned and shocked with the brutality with the british. 

  • RNT returned the title of knighthood and Gandhi returned title of Kaiser-i-Hind.
  • Deenbandhu CF Andrews went on to say вАЬThis was the most heinous crime against humanity that he has ever seenвАЭ.
  • British government appointed Hunter commission to look into the brutality byt it gave a very lack-luster report. It did not prescribe any punishment to General Dyre. And Lt. Governor of Punjab Michael o Dyre. It just gave a soft warning to them
  • Meanwhile in Britain General Dyre was celebrated as hero. In house of lords he was hailed as a Lion who saved british empire in India.
  • Moreover the Morning Post newspaper organization collected 30000 pound for General Dyre. 

Later the revenge of this killing was taken by Udham Singh, who assassinated Michael o Dyre in 1940.

# Khilafat issue and NCM:#

In the WW1, indian muslim sepoys were given assurance by british authority that after the end of war they will not disturb the position of Khalifa (Sultan of Turkey was considered as spiritual leader of Islam and he was given that title).

They also assured that authority of Khalifa on sacred religious places of Islam will remain intact. 

After the end of war, B's broke their promise and removed Khalifa from the position of sultan form Turkey. 

At this time in Indian Khilafat committee was constituted under the leadership of Md. Ali, Shaukat Ali, Hasrat Mohani, Abul Kalam Azad, Ajmal Kham.

They demanded:

  1. B's must restore position of Khalifa.
  2. After territorial rearrangement, Khalifa must be left with enough territory so that he can exercise control over holy religious places of Islam.

Later Gandhi also became member of khilafat committee and he also went on to become president of committee. he advocated for khilafat non cooperation movement to address the issue of:

  1. Indian Muslims
  2. and rectification of Punjab wrongs.

Moreover there was very high dis-satisfaction with GoI Act 1919.

Special Session of INC 1920 (Calcutta):

  • It was presided by LLR {the 1st president from extremist side}.
  • Gandhi proposed his idea of NC and went on to claim that if NC is initiated then indian will get its freedom within 1 year.
  • Gandhi was vehemently opposed by CR Das. Moreover LLR also did not agreed with Gandhi.
  • Though majority of the people supported Gandhi's idea of NC but final decision was left for annual session to be held at Nagpur.

Annual Session of INC 1920 (Nagpur):

  • It was presided by Vijay Raghava Cheriyar. In this session NC related proposals were moved by CR Das and Gandhi made proposals related to Congress organization.
  • NCM related proposals:
    • Boycott of foreign goods and services, foreign titles, british administered educational institutes, courts, visits of High officials and monarchs from Britain
    • Promotion and to spun khadi, distribution of charkha.
    • Boycott of central and provincial legislative councils.
  • Congress related proposals:
    • Gandhi proposed the things related to bringing more effectiveness in the functioning of congress organization
      1. creation of CWC to look into day to day affairs of congress.
      2. creation of provincial congress committees on linguistic basis.
      3. creation of all India congress committee to coordinate b/n provincial committees.
      4. As extent possible create congress committees in villages and towns.
      5. Gandhi further recommended the annual subscription of congress or membership fee must be reduced to 25 paise so as congress reaches to even countryside of India.
      6. As extent possible develop Hindi written in Devanagari script as a contact language in provinces.

Development during NCM:

  1. Boycott of foreign goods, services, titles, educational institutes and courts was very successful.
  2. People like Rajendra Prasad, C Rajagopalachari, and Sardar Patel left the practice of law for sometime.
  3. Khadi and charkha started to emerge as National symbol.
  4. In 1921, Tilak Swaraj Fund was created and within a short span of time, 1cr rupees was collected in the fund.
  5. Women enthusiastically contributed to this fund by giving their jewelry. Even many courtesan's contributed to swaraj fund.
  6. In this duration there was foundation of 4 prominent educational institutes i.e. Kashi Vidyapeeth, Bihar Vidyapeeth, Gujarat Vidyapeeth, and Jamia Millia Islamia.
  7. Meanwhile MMM facilitated talks b/n Gandhi and viceroy Reading. But talks failed.
  8. In 1922, Prince of Wales arrived in India which led to huge protest and movement in bombay and also violence on the streets of Bombay. Gandhi observed one week fast to control the violence.
  9. Gandhi also gave ultimatum to british authority that if political prisoners are not released within a week, then he will initiate civil disobedience from Bardoli.

Chauri-Chaura Incident:

  • A retired british indian army soldier Bhagwan Ahir initiated a protest form Gauri Bazaar against high inflation in food.
  • On 4th Feb 1922, he was joined by 2500 protesters. Police officials opened fire which led to killing of 3 ppl.
  • As such masses retaliated and they torched the police station. which led to killing of 22 police men. All of them were indians.

After chauri Chaura incident, Gandhi decided to call off NCM from Bardoli Resolution 12th Feb. British authority arrested Gandhi on the charges of sedition and he was sent to jail for 6 years.

At this time Gandhi was criticized for calling off NCM when it was going good. Moreover some nationalist went on to say that why entire nation will pay the cost of violence to few

Gandhi replied that if he does not condemns the violence of chauri Chaura incident then it will be an inspiration or provocation to others to indulge in violence.

At this time Gandhi favored the strategy of Struggle-Truce-Struggle (STS). He was of the view that masses cannot sacrifice endlessly and after an intense period of struggle there is a need of period of truce in which masses can rejuvenate and reprepare for the next bigger phase of struggle.

Analysis of NCM:

  • NCM made congress as party of masses and it also improved reach of congress to the hinterland of India.
  • It created connecting link b/n the masses and the nationalist in the rural areas.
  • Congress realized the true potential of India lies with masses in the rural areas and effectiveness of any mass movement depends on their participation.
  • For a shorter period of time there was complete cooperation b/n H's and M's.
  • From NCM huge participation of women in national movement started. {because of the nature of protest was changed вЖТ Non violence вЖТ they can participate w/o loss of dignity and can contribute equally as that of men}

Criticism of NCM:

  •  Great participation of educated middle class Muslims in NCM was on the basis of anti-imperial religious consciousness. But congress as an organization failed to convert this consciousness into politico-secular consciousness.

By the end of 1923, Khilafat issue also lost its relevance. People of turkey rose against Khalifa and under the leadership of Mustafa Kamal Pasha they removed sultan of turkey or Khalifa for his position and it emerged as secular democratic state.

# Phase of Swarajist (1920 -1926):#

After Gandhi's arrest a sense of confusion started to prevail in congress. Leaders like CRD and MLN wanted the change in strategy of congress.

In 1922, annual session of congress held at Gaya and it was presided by CRD. In this session he proposed to end the boycott of councils and enter into provincial and legislative councils.

He was backed by MLN and they came to be known as Pro-changers. They further said they are not giving up hope on NCM in fact once they enter into councils they will extend NCM into councils by hindering each and every work of council. {end or mend}

OTOH, Leaders like SVP, C Rajagopalachari, Rajendra Prasad opposed council entry and advice the constructive work suggested by Gandhi.

  1. Upliftment of backwards and women's
  2. Build H-M unity
  3. Removal of UTy.

Since they oppose council entry they came to be known as no-changers. As a result on 1st Jan 1923 CRD and MLN declared creation of Khilafat Swaraj Party with CRD as president and MLN as secretary after some point of time both the factions realized that their differences will cause harm to national interest {also feared a 1907 like split} and started to find common grounds of cooperation which is as follows:

  • However important council work may be, it cannot replace the mass movement 
  • There was no immediate environment of mass movement.
  • Swarajist / Prochangers can be given a chance to enter into the councils till the environment for mass movement is created.

Gandhi's stand on council entry: {вЖТexpressed in Belgaum Session 1924 where he was president}

  • He was released from jail in Feb. 1924. 
  • He was completely against council entry and believed that it must not have happened at the very first place but he did not opposed council entry in 1924 because it has already happened. 
  • and he felt opposing at this time will be seen as weakness of congress organization and it will also bring embarrassment to the nationalist.

Achievements of Swarajist:

  • In the elections 1923, Swarajist performed very well in province of Bombay, Bengal, central province and united province
  • they won 38 out of 101 seats in Central Assembly. {overall 41 as included nominated}
  • While being in council they worked in many fronts like:
    • They advocated more freedom of speech and expression.
    • Development of indigenous industries.
    • To convene a round table conference for the purpose of reviewing GoI Act 1919 and to go ahead with future constitutional reforms
    • They defeated Public Safety bill which was aimed at curbing the activities of communist and socialist leaders.

Gandhi was not very much satisfied with the council entry work. When british PM Stanley Baldwin declared the formation of Simon Commission, Swarajist merged with main congress to work in the direction of mass movement.

In 1925, Swarajist were successful in electing the Vithalbhai Patel as the 1st indian speaker of central legislative council.

Responsivist:

  • Leaders like LLR, MMM, MR Jayakar decided that they will not resign form the Assembly and they will work the reforms in whatever capacity they have been given in the councils.
